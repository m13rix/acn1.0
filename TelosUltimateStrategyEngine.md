# FINAL STRATEGY: OGE Preparation for Subject 13

## Strategy choice
Use a **heavy-priority rotation**:
- **Math and Russian** get the largest share of the week.
- **Informatics and English** stay on low-cost maintenance.
- **Monday is lighter**.
- No subject runs for the whole day.
- Study is broken into rotating blocks to prevent cognitive bleed.

This is the best surviving chain from the route exploration:
- **Heavy-Priority Rotation** kept as the top weekly operating model.
- Day roles kept: **Russian-heavy day**, **Math-heavy day**, **mixed maintenance day**, **lighter recovery day**.
- `simulation day` and `review day` were treated as overlays, not standalone day roles.

---

## Weekly principles
1. **Russian is the highest-risk subject.**
    - It has the worst gaps.
    - It needs repeated weak-topic work, not broad generic exposure.
2. **Math is the second priority.**
    - It needs timed sets plus correction.
3. **Informatics is mostly maintenance.**
    - Current level is already strong.
4. **English is mostly maintenance.**
    - Keep it warm, don't overinvest.
5. **Rotation is mandatory.**
    - Switch subjects inside the day so attention stays fresh.
6. **Weekend is for deeper correction and checkpoints.**
    - Weekdays are for targeted execution.

## Day-by-day schedule

### Monday — lighter recovery day
**Goal:** keep the week alive without draining energy.

**Time window:** start ~15:40, total ~1.5–2.0 hours.

- **15:40–16:20** — Russian weak-topic microset
    - 1 short topic cluster or 1 mini-variant set.
    - Focus: one known weak area only.
- **16:30–17:05** — English maintenance
    - grammar, reading, or short vocabulary set.
- **17:05–17:15** — log errors and stop

**Monday rule:** do not push into heavy math or long Russian marathons. The point is to recover and keep momentum.

---

### Tuesday — Russian-heavy day
**Goal:** attack the biggest gap early in the week.

**Time window:** start ~15:10, total ~3–3.5 hours.

- **15:10–16:15** — Russian weak-topic block
    - 2–3 test variants on one weak topic group.
    - If accuracy is low, stop adding new variants and fix the error pattern.
- **16:25–17:10** — Russian correction block
    - redo misses, write the rule, restate the pattern.
- **17:20–18:10** — Math maintenance block
    - 1 medium problem set or 1 mini-variant.
- **18:10–18:25** — quick recap + error log

**Tuesday rule:** Russian is the main subject, but Math gets a short touch so it does not disappear.

---

### Wednesday — Math-heavy day
**Goal:** move Math from 4 toward 5 through timed practice and correction.

**Time window:** start ~15:10, total ~3–3.5 hours.

- **15:10–16:15** — Math timed set
    - one focused topic set, or one mini-variant under time.
- **16:25–17:10** — Math correction block
    - review every error and classify it.
- **17:20–18:00** — Russian maintenance block
    - 1 short Russian drill or 1 weak-topic recap.
- **18:00–18:20** — English or Informatics micro-maintenance
    - keep the strong subjects warm.

**Wednesday rule:** Math gets the best energy window; Russian stays present so it does not cool off.

---

### Thursday — mixed maintenance + Russian repair
**Goal:** prevent drift in the strong subjects while keeping repair pressure on Russian.

**Time window:** start ~15:10, total ~3 hours.

- **15:10–16:00** — Russian repair block
    - 1–2 variants on the same weak pattern.
- **16:10–16:55** — Informatics maintenance
    - current informatics level is strong, so use compact maintenance only.
- **17:05–17:45** — Math mixed drill
    - mixed tasks, not a full marathon.
- **17:45–18:00** — English maintenance or error log

**Thursday rule:** keep the day mixed, but do not let maintenance steal the whole session.

---

### Friday — priority alternation / consolidation
**Goal:** close the school week with a strong repeat on both weak subjects.

**Time window:** start ~15:10, total ~3–3.5 hours.

- **15:10–16:05** — Math timed mini-variant
- **16:15–17:05** — Russian timed mini-variant
- **17:15–17:50** — correction of both sessions
- **17:50–18:10** — quick Informatics or English touch

**Friday rule:** Friday is for consolidation, not for inventing new topics.

---

### Saturday — deep work + diagnostic checkpoint
**Goal:** use the longer weekend window for the hardest testing and correction.

**Time window:** start ~13:00, total ~3.5–4 hours.

- **13:00–14:10** — Russian diagnostic mini-mock
    - 2–3 variants or one timed block from the weakest area.
- **14:20–15:10** — Russian correction + voice review if needed
- **15:20–16:10** — Math timed set
- **16:20–16:50** — English or Informatics maintenance
- **16:50–17:00** — weekly notes

**Saturday rule:** this is the best day for voice-agent help and serious correction.

---

### Sunday — correction + weekly reset
**Goal:** stabilize the week, fix leftovers, and set up the next one.

**Time window:** start ~12:30, total ~3.5–4 hours.

- **12:30–13:20** — Math correction block
- **13:30–14:20** — Russian correction block
- **14:30–15:00** — Informatics maintenance
- **15:10–15:40** — English maintenance
- **15:40–16:00** — weekly review and next-week plan

**Sunday rule:** finish with a clear list of what failed, what improved, and what gets repeated next week.

## Test-variant targets per session

### Russian
- **Weak-topic block:** 2–3 variants on one narrow topic group.
- **Correction block:** 1 variant fully corrected, plus error rule notes.
- **Weekend diagnostic:** 3 variants maximum if focus stays high.
- If the topic is truly weak, do fewer variants and more correction.

### Math
- **Timed block:** 1 mini-variant or 8–12 tasks on one topic family.
- **Correction block:** redo misses immediately.
- **Weekend mixed drill:** 1–2 mixed sets if accuracy is stable.

### Informatics
- **Maintenance block:** 1 short task set or one focused task family.
- Use current success on tasks 1–4 as proof that maintenance is enough unless a new weakness appears.

### English
- **Maintenance block:** short grammar/reading/listening/vocabulary set.
- The goal is to keep the skill warm, not to overtrain it.

## Voice agent deployment triggers
Use the voice agent with screen view when it has real leverage:

1. **Stuck for 10 minutes on one error.**
    - If the same mistake repeats, switch to voice help.
2. **Russian explanation needed.**
    - Best use case: weak grammar logic, wording, or rule confusion.
3. **Math solution path is unclear.**
    - Use screen view to inspect the problem and walk through the reasoning.
4. **After a timed block with low accuracy.**
    - The voice agent helps convert errors into rules.
5. **Before or after weekend diagnostic work.**
    - Best moment for deeper explanation and fix.
6. **When editing a website or checking answer formatting.**
    - Use screen view to verify what is actually on the page.

**Do not** use the voice agent for every task. Save it for high-friction moments.

## Weekend vs weekday differential

### Weekdays
- Shorter, sharper, more focused.
- Usually 2–3 subject blocks.
- More likely to be single-topic repair.
- Monday is intentionally lighter.

### Weekend
- Longer blocks and deeper correction.
- Better for diagnostics, mini-mocks, and voice-assisted review.
- More suitable for mixed sets and weekly reset planning.
- Use the extra time to close the loop on mistakes from the week.

## Progress tracking checkpoints

### Daily checkpoint
At the end of every study day, record:
- subjects studied,
- number of variants/problems completed,
- number of repeated errors,
- one mistake rule learned,
- one next-step priority.

### Weekly checkpoint
Every Sunday, answer:
1. Did Russian weak-topic accuracy improve?
2. Did Math timed performance improve?
3. Did Informatics stay stable?
4. Did English stay stable?
5. Which topics still repeat errors?

### Red flags
Trigger extra correction if:
- the same Russian mistake appears twice in one week,
- Math timed accuracy falls twice in a row,
- a session ends with no clear error rule,
- Monday becomes too heavy and causes the week to collapse.

### Green flags
The plan is working if:
- Russian errors shrink week to week,
- Math becomes more stable under time pressure,
- strong subjects stay easy to maintain,
- study starts feel less costly and more automatic.

---

## Fallback rules
- If energy is very low, keep the day but cut the load.
- If a session is missed, do not punish the next day; resume the planned rotation.
- If one subject keeps failing, give it the next high-energy slot and use the voice agent.
- If Russian improves faster than expected, shift a little time from Russian to Math, but do not flatten the priority structure.

---

## Final recommendation
The best weekly strategy for Subject 13 is:
- **Heavy-priority rotation**
- **Russian-heavy and Math-heavy weekday focus**
- **Light Monday**
- **Weekend correction and checkpointing**
- **Minimal maintenance for Informatics and English**
- **Voice agent only on high-value friction points**

This plan is specific, repeatable, and aimed directly at the actual bottlenecks.
