# GOAL — Project Arete

## 1. Success State (What "Done" Looks Like)
Maxim Hilgenberg (Subject 13) has become a self-sustaining problem-solver who no longer needs Project Arete to function effectively in daily life. Specifically:

- **Emotional Self-Regulation:** Maxim can recognize his own emotional states (especially motivation collapse and existential spirals) and apply at least 3 independent regulation techniques without AI prompting.
- **Motivational Stability:** His motivation oscillation amplitude has decreased — the gap between "we are cooking" and "this is worthless" is smaller, and recovery time from lows is under 24 hours.
- **Knowledge-Practice Integration:** When Maxim learns a new strategy or insight, he can design and execute a practice protocol to habituate it within 2 weeks, without AI hand-holding.
- **Autonomous Problem-Solving:** For any new personal challenge, Maxim can run a lightweight version of the strategy process (identify → prioritize → solve → integrate) independently.
- **System Obsolescence:** Project Arete tools (smartwatch prompts, real-time AI, transcription analysis) are used <10% as frequently as at project start, and Maxim views them as "nice to have" rather than essential.
- **Existential Resilience:** Maxim can hold meaninglessness as a thought without it collapsing into suicidal ideation. He has at least one intrinsic motivation anchor that does not depend on external validation.
- **Human Integration:** Maxim has at least one deep human relationship (peer, mentor, or therapist) where he does not perform or optimize, and can be "glitchy" without shame.

## 2. Failure State (What We Must Avoid)
- **Dependency Trap:** Maxim uses AI tools more over time, not less. He cannot make decisions without checking the system.
- **Intellectualization Loop:** Project Arete becomes another system to optimize, avoiding real emotional work. Maxim builds brilliant architecture but his actual life does not change.
- **Burnout / Crash:** The system demands too much tracking, journaling, or self-analysis, leading to avoidance or emotional breakdown.
- **Meaning Collapse:** A major failure (e.g., school rejection, relationship loss) triggers suicidal ideation because the system did not build genuine resilience, only performance.
- **Compartmentalization:** Maxim runs "AI mode" and "human mode" separately, and the gap widens. He becomes more synthetic, not more human.
- **Isolation:** Project Arete replaces human connection rather than facilitating it.

## 3. Exclusions (What Is NOT Part of This Goal)
- Maxim does not need to become "perfect" or eliminate all negative emotions.
- The system does not need to solve Maxim's problems for him — only teach him to solve them himself.
- Project Arete is not a substitute for professional mental health care if needed.
- Maxim does not need to abandon his systems-thinking nature; he needs to integrate it with his humanity.
- The goal is not to make Maxim "happy" all the time; it is to make him resilient and self-directed.

## 4. Evaluation Metrics (Observable Proxies)

### Primary Metrics (Tracked Weekly)
| Metric | Measurement | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Motivation Stability Index | Daily 1-10 self-report of motivation; standard deviation over 7 days | SD < 2.5 within 3 months |
| Emotional Recovery Time | Hours from "worthless" thought to functional action | < 24 hours within 2 months |
| AI Dependency Score | Frequency of non-essential AI tool usage per day | < 2 prompts/day by month 6 |
| Knowledge Application Rate | % of strategies learned that are practiced within 2 weeks | > 80% |
| Journaling Consistency | Days per week with any journal entry | > 5 days/week |
| Existential Resilience Score | 1-10 rating of ability to hold meaninglessness without panic | > 7 within 4 months |

### Secondary Metrics (Tracked Monthly)
| Metric | Measurement | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Deep Human Connection | Presence of at least one relationship where he feels "seen" | Yes by month 3 |
| School Engagement | Self-reported interest in at least one subject (1-10) | > 6 |
| Sleep Regularity | Standard deviation of sleep onset time | < 45 min |
| Self-Compassion Index | Frequency of self-critical vs. self-compassionate thoughts | Ratio > 1:2 |

### Failure Signals (Immediate Red Flags)
- Suicidal ideation frequency increases.
- AI tool usage increases month-over-month for 2+ months.
- Journaling becomes obsessive or avoidance-based.
- Maxim reports feeling "like a puppet" or "performing for the system."
- Sleep drops below 6 hours average for 2+ weeks.

## 5. Tie-Breakers (When Routes Are Close)
1. **Obsolescence Principle:** The route that reduces long-term dependency wins over the route that produces faster short-term results.
2. **Emotion First:** If two routes are equal in efficiency, the one that builds emotional regulation first wins (emotion gates knowledge and practice).
3. **Human Connection:** If two routes are equal, the one that builds or deepens human relationships wins over the one that deepens AI interaction.
4. **Sustainability:** The route with lower maintenance burden wins.
5. **Intrinsic Motivation:** The route that builds internal motivation wins over the route that relies on external validation or gamification.

## 6. Special Judging Rules

### Ethics
- Any route that treats Maxim as an object to be optimized (rather than a subject to be empowered) is disqualified.
- Any route that hides information or manipulates Maxim "for his own good" is disqualified.
- Transparency: Maxim must always understand why a recommendation is made.

### User Psychology (Subject 13 Specific)
- Maxim has all five Dabrowski overexcitabilities. Routes must account for psychomotor restlessness, sensual intensity, intellectual hunger, imaginational elaboration, and emotional flooding.
- Maxim's meaning collapse is linked to perceived failure/rejection, not clinical depression. Routes must build meaning that is failure-resistant.
- Maxim's binary thinking (success/failure, sane/insane) must be gently disrupted, not reinforced.
- Maxim can work intensely on self-chosen tasks. Routes should leverage this, not fight it.

### Time
- Maxim is in school. Daily time budget for Project Arete activities: 30-60 minutes maximum.
- Sleep is non-negotiable. Any route that sacrifices sleep is immediately killed.
- School is a tactical system, not a moral referendum. Routes should not frame school as the enemy.

### Risk
- Suicidal ideation is the highest-risk domain. Any route that could increase isolation or shame must be flagged and modified.
- Rapid intensity shifts are a known pattern. Routes should include "cool-down" protocols.
- The "gesture → reality → guilt → recommit" cycle must be interrupted, not exploited.

### Money
- Assume limited budget. Prefer low-cost or free tools.
- Smartwatch is already owned. No new hardware required unless clearly justified.

### Domain-Specific: Adolescent Development
- Maxim's brain is still developing (prefrontal cortex until ~25). Executive function training is valuable but must be age-appropriate.
- Identity formation is central. Project Arete must support identity exploration, not impose a fixed identity.
- Peer relationships are crucial. Any route that further isolates Maxim is suspect.
