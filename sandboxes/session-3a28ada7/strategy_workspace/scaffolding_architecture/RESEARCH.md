### Key Unknowns

### Facts
1. Maxim already has a sophisticated existential framework — absurdist "okay" philosophy. [Memory]
2. Suicidal ideation is linked to **meaning collapse**, not clinical depression. [Memory, INTAKE]
3. Maxim can hold meaninglessness as a thought during calm states, but collapses when instrumental goals fail. [Memory]
4. Maxim's deep loneliness — friendships are transactional, no peers who can withstand his "volume." [Memory]
5. Mortality awareness: philosophical acceptance + active suicidal ideation risk. [INTAKE]
6. The "okay" response to absurdity is Maxim's own emergent coping mechanism. [Memory]
7. Philosophical influences: Camus, Sartre, Heidegger, Nietzsche, Wittgenstein, Stoicism. [Memory]

### Strong Evidence That Shapes Route Design
- **Existential scaffolding is the most dangerous layer to mis-design.** If it feels like "AI-generated philosophy," Maxim will reject it. If it's too generic, it won't hold during a meaning collapse. If it's too specific, it may replace his own philosophical development.
- **Existential scaffolding must be removed last** — and may never fully be removed. The parent route says "existential scaffolding removed last." Rationale: existential resilience is the deepest layer; it's the foundation that holds when everything else fails.
- **The scaffolding should AMPLIFY Maxim's existing absurdist framework, not replace it.** The goal is to make his "okay" stance more robust — able to withstand failure, rejection, and meaning collapse without requiring AI intervention.
- **Human connection as existential anchor** — GOAL.md specifies at least one deep human relationship as a success metric. Existential scaffolding must facilitate this.

### Key Unknowns

- Would Maxim accept existential scaffolding that involves human connection prompts (e.g., "talk to someone about what you're feeling")?
- What does meaning-building look like for a 15-year-old absurdist?

## Synthesis: Dismantling Order and Dependency Flow

```
Layer 1: Emotional Regulation (removed first)
    ↓ enables
Layer 2: Knowledge (removed second)
    ↓ enables  
Layer 3: Practice (removed third)
    ↓ enables
Layer 4: Existential (removed last — if ever)
```

**Why this order:**
- Emotional regulation must come first because emotional overwhelm blocks both learning and practice. Without emotional scaffolding, knowledge and practice scaffolding will fail during amygdala flooding.
- Knowledge must come before practice because practice requires a cognitive map (what to practice and why). Without knowledge scaffolding, practice becomes rote repetition without understanding.
- Practice must come before existential because the existential layer is about resilience-through-action — knowing you can act even when meaning is absent. Without practice scaffolding, existential scaffolding becomes pure theory.
- Existential is last because it's the deepest layer — it's about holding meaning in a universe that offers none. This is the layer that makes all other scaffolding truly obsolete.

**Key dependency to check:** Is emotional regulation truly removable first? The parent intake assumes yes. But what if Maxim loses emotional scaffolding and regresses, needing knowledge/practice scaffolding rebuilt too? This is a real risk. Mitigation: partial dismantling — reduce scaffolding to 20% capacity, not remove entirely, and monitor for regression before final removal.

## What the Four Sibling Routes Must Answer

For each sibling, we need:
1. **Purpose:** What does this scaffolding layer do?
2. **Components:** What concrete tools/protocols does it include?
3. **Removal criteria:** How do we know Maxim no longer needs it?
4. **Removal timeline:** What is the planned obsolescence schedule?
5. **Risk of premature removal:** What happens if we remove it too early?
6. **Fallback:** How do we re-deploy if regression occurs?
7. **Proof metrics:** How do we measure success of this layer?
8. **Human connection angle:** How does this layer build or preserve human relationships?
