## What-if checks

**What if no therapist is available or affordable?**
→ Shift to mentor model (school counselor, community mentor, older peer) or peer deepening protocol. Still viable but lower clinical safety coverage.

**What if Maxim refuses human contact due to shame?**
→ Use AI as intermediary — role-play conversations, prepare scripts, build confidence gradually. This is a longer timeline but still viable.

**What if this works and completely reframes the problem?**
→ Best case: Maxim develops a deep human relationship that becomes his primary emotional anchor. This is the ultimate GOAL.md success condition. But it is not a "route" in the scaffolding sense — it is a life outcome.

**What if one permission flips it?**
→ If parents approve therapy immediately, execution likelihood jumps. If school counselor is available, mentor path opens.

**What if stronger preparation makes it viable?**
→ Preparation: research 3 therapists in Maxim's area, prepare parent conversation script, identify potential natural mentors. This would improve execution likelihood.

**What if this route is bad as a final route but excellent as an information-gathering opener?**
→ It IS an information-gathering route. Testing Maxim's support network, family dynamics, and vulnerability capacity is essential. But the information can be gathered without a dedicated Human Bridge route — through conversation, journaling prompts, and parent check-ins.

## Confidence
**Low-Moderate (55%)** — Critical for GOAL.md success but highest execution barriers, slowest timeline, and most external dependencies. The unmet need is real, but the path to meeting it is uncertain.

## Why kill is justified
1. **Not a scaffolding tool:** Human Bridge is a life outcome, not a temporary scaffold. It does not fit the obsolescence model — it transforms rather than retires. This makes it conceptually different from Music Ladder, Speech Protocol, and Breathing App.
2. **Highest execution barriers:** Finding a therapist/mentor, overcoming shame, initiating contact, and building trust take weeks to months. Music Ladder can show results in days.
3. **External dependencies:** Requires therapist availability, financial resources, parental consent, and Maxim's willingness — all outside Project Arete's direct control.
4. **Can be addressed outside the route tree:** The core function (human connection) is better addressed through:
   - Journaling prompts about relationships
   - Parent conversations about therapy
   - Peer interaction experiments
   - These can be embedded within Music Ladder (lyric analysis about connection) or Practice Scaffolding (social skill practice).
5. **GOAL.md already mandates it:** "Deep human connection" is a success metric, not a scaffolding layer. It should be tracked as a cross-cutting metric, not a dedicated route.
6. **Safety coverage is real but addressable:** Suicidal ideation escalation can be handled through crisis protocols (embedded in the system architecture) without a dedicated Human Bridge route.

**Note:** Human Bridge should be reconceptualized as a cross-cutting GOAL.md success metric and a feature of other routes (e.g., "connection check-ins" within journaling, "social practice" within Practice Scaffolding) rather than a standalone depth-4 route under Emotional Regulation Scaffolding.
