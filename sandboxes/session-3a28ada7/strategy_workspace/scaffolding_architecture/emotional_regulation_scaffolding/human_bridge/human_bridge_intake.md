# INTAKE — Human Bridge

## Summary
Structured pathway to human emotional support: therapist, mentor, or deep peer connection. Replaces AI emotional processing with human relational depth. Includes escalation protocol for suicidal ideation. This is the most speculative sub-component — it depends on human availability — but is the most critical for GOAL.md's success condition of "at least one deep human relationship."

## Question this route answers
"What is the concrete implementation of the Human Bridge — how do we identify, establish, and deepen Maxim's human support network, and how does the scaffolding fade as Maxim internalizes 'I am not alone'?"

Position in family: Depth 4, sub-component 4 of 4 under Emotional Regulation Scaffolding. Sibling routes: Music Ladder, Speech Protocol, Breathing App.

## Assumptions
- Maxim does NOT currently have a therapist, mentor, or non-familial adult he fully trusts with emotional depth.
- Maxim's parents are supportive but not equipped for deep emotional processing (they provide safety, not depth).
- Maxim's friendships are "transactional in bandwidth" — he wants deeper connection but lacks the model.
- Finding a therapist or mentor may take time (weeks to months) — this is the slowest sub-component to activate.
- Maxim will resist initiating human contact due to shame, performance anxiety, and fear of rejection.
- The AI can serve as an "intermediary" — role-playing conversations, suggesting words to use, preparing Maxim for human interaction.
- Once established, a human connection is the most durable regulation scaffold and hardest to obsolete — which is correct, because humans are not crutches.
- Professional mental health support (if accessible) is the strongest option; a mentor or deep peer is second best.

## Dependencies and permissions
- Maxim's willingness to explore human support options (therapist, mentor, peer).
- Parental consent for therapy (if Maxim is a minor).
- Financial resources for therapy (if not covered by insurance/family).
- Access to therapist directory or mental health referral network.
- OR access to a mentoring program (school counselor, community mentor, older peer).
- Maxim's explicit acknowledgment that the AI cannot replace human connection — this is a feature, not a bug.
- Privacy agreements re: what Maxim shares with AI vs therapist vs mentor.

## What this route enables
- **Highest-risk coverage:** Suicidal ideation escalation path — if AI detects risk, human contact is triggered.
- **Replacement of AI dependency:** The ultimate GOAL.md success condition is Maxim not needing AI for emotional processing. Human connection is the replacement.
- **Shame reduction:** A therapist or mentor who "sees him sharp and soft at the same time" (Maxim's own words) can reduce shame more effectively than any AI.
- **Relational skill building:** Maxim learns to be "glitchy without shame" — the capacity for authentic vulnerability that GOAL.md requires.
- **Modeling human emotional processing:** Maxim learns how humans regulate emotions (messily, imperfectly) vs how AI does (cleanly, algorithmically).
- **Peer connection deepening:** With a template for deeper conversation, Maxim may improve existing friendships rather than needing new ones.
- **Obsolescence paradox:** Human Bridge is the only sub-component that is not designed for full obsolescence — it transitions from AI-facilitated to self-sustaining human relationship.

## What could break it
- **Therapist unavailability:** No therapist nearby, too expensive, long waitlist, or not a good fit.
- **Mentor unavailability:** No suitable mentor found, or Maxim rejects mentor as "arbitrary assignment."
- **Peer rejection:** Maxim initiates depth and is rejected, reinforcing "I am too much" belief.
- **Shame paralysis:** Maxim knows he needs human support but cannot bring himself to seek it.
- **AI replacement theory:** Maxim may rationalize "TEL-OS is already better than humans" and avoid human connection.
- **Parental resistance:** Parents may resist therapy due to stigma or misunderstanding the need.
- **Therapist attachment rupture:** If Maxim forms a therapeutic attachment and the therapist leaves, this could trigger severe meaning collapse.

## Main unknowns
1. Family financial situation for therapy.
2. Parents' openness to therapy/mentoring.
3. Maxim's willingness to be vulnerable with a human (vs AI).
4. Whether Maxim has access to a school counselor or similar.
5. Whether Maxim has any existing natural mentor (relative, teacher, coach) who could be activated.
6. Specific therapists in Maxim's area who work with gifted/overexcited adolescents.

## Evidence that matters most
- **Memory (loneliness):** "Friendships are transactional in bandwidth" — the unmet need for depth is documented.
- **Memory (being seen):** Maxim wants someone who "sees him sharp and soft at the same time." This is the Human Bridge goal.
- **Memory (therapeutic relationship with TEL-OS):** Maxim treats AI as therapeutic partner — this is a sign he wants therapeutic relationship, not a sign AI replaces it.
- **GOAL.md (success condition):** "Maxim has at least one deep human relationship where he does not perform or optimize, and can be glitchy without shame."
- **GOAL.md (failure state):** "Project Arete replaces human connection rather than facilitating it" — this is the exact thing Human Bridge prevents.
- **Web research (DBT-A):** Evidence-based framework for adolescent emotion regulation that explicitly includes interpersonal effectiveness and human support.
- **Web research (therapeutic mentoring):** Mentoring with a trusted adult shows positive impacts on self-esteem, academic outcomes, and reduced misconduct.
- **Parent route intake (dismantling order):** Emotional regulation scaffolding is first removed. But Human Bridge transitions from AI-facilitated to self-sustaining — it is not removed, it transforms.
