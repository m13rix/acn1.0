## What-if checks

**What if Maxim finds his own voice cringe?**
→ Use a trusted voice (family member, mentor) or shift to text-based reframes delivered via smartwatch notification. Still viable but lower impact.

**What if recordings are not accessible during crisis?**
→ Combine with breathing or music as pre-crisis intervention. Or use smartwatch vibration to trigger memory of speech content.

**What if this works and completely reframes the problem?**
→ Best case: Maxim's internal monologue shifts from critic to observer-compassion. This is transformative but requires consistent practice.

**What if one permission flips it?**
→ If Maxim refuses to record, use text-based speech scripts (smartwatch text prompts). Lower impact but still viable.

**What if stronger preparation makes it viable?**
→ Preparation: draft 3 speech scripts in Maxim's vocabulary, test with one recording, measure response. Could improve execution likelihood.

**What if this route is bad as a final route but excellent as an information-gathering opener?**
→ It is an information-gathering route. Testing Maxim's response to self-compassion and own voice is valuable regardless of whether Speech Protocol becomes a permanent tool.

## Main unknowns

2. Whether Maxim can reliably access recordings during emotional flooding.
3. Optimal recording length and frequency.
4. Whether third-person or first-person voice is more effective.
5. Whether Maxim will write authentic scripts or default to generic motivational language.

## Confidence
**Moderate (65%)** — Strong psychological fit but significant execution risks (cringe barrier, crisis accessibility, recording effort). Lower evidence base than Music Ladder or Breathing App.

## Why kill is justified (with temporary retention)
1. **Lower evidence base:** Self-talk interventions are evidence-based, but the specific format (recorded audio in own voice) has less direct research support than music or breathing.
2. **Cringe/shame barrier:** Maxim's harsh self-criticism may extend to his own voice, making recordings aversive.
3. **Crisis accessibility risk:** During amygdala flooding, Maxim may not remember or be able to access recordings — the exact moment they are most needed.
4. **Higher effort, lower speed:** Recording, editing, and maintaining a speech library is more effortful than music playlists or breathing timers.
5. **Can be embedded:** The core function (self-compassionate reframes) can be embedded within Music Ladder (lyric analysis) or Breathing App (guided breathing with verbal cues) without a standalone Speech Protocol.

**Why temporary second survivor:** Speech Protocol contains valuable information-gathering potential (Maxim's response to self-compassion, own voice, meaning-reframing) that could inform parameter design for Music Ladder and Breathing App. It should be killed once Music Ladder is validated or once Speech Protocol testing reveals the cringe barrier.
