### Channel 2: Web Research

#### Music Ladder (ISO Principle)
- **FACT**: ISO Principle is the evidence-based gold standard — match current mood state with music, then gradually shift toward desired state. (positivepsychology.com, capkids.org, replenishhere.com)
- **FACT**: "Tuned In" program shows significant pre-to-post improvements in emotion awareness, identification, and regulation for at-risk adolescents (p < 0.01). 98% attendance rate. (Frontiers in Psychology, 2016)
- **FACT**: Empowering music interventions significantly increase positive emotions after stress; calming music reduces stress scores. (Fountain, 2023 dissertation)
- **FACT**: Music activates dopamine release, modulates amygdala, and regulates cortisol. (multiple sources)
- **FACT**: Music therapy for adolescents with EBD shows strongest evidence for internalizing outcomes (anxiety, depression) and social-emotional skills. (Rodwin et al., 2022 systematic review)
- **FACT**: Gender and personal music preferences significantly affect emotional responses to music. (Wei et al., 2025)
- **STRUCTURE**: 15-minute blocks, 3-5 songs, mood check before/after, guided listening with bodily sensation focus.

#### Breathing App (Paced Respiration)
- **FACT**: Slow-paced breathing at 5-6 bpm significantly increases HRV, vagal tone, and parasympathetic activation. (Little, 2025 — A52 Breath Method review)
- **FACT**: The A52 method (5s inhale, 5s exhale, 2s hold) aligns with 67% of effective breathing techniques studied. (Little, 2025)
- **FACT**: Diaphragmatic breathing reduces cortisol, blood pressure, and improves emotional regulation in adolescents. (Tsakona et al., 2025 — PMC systematic review)
- **FACT**: Box breathing (4-4-4-4), 4-7-8 breathing, and psychological sigh (double inhale + long exhale) are evidence-based techniques for teens. (missionprephealthcare.com)
- **FACT**: Slow-breathing curriculum for high school students shows feasibility and preliminary effectiveness. (Bentley et al., 2022)
- **FACT**: 5 minutes of diaphragmatic breathing can significantly lower perceived stress. (multiple RCTs)
- **FACT**: Breathing stimulates vagus nerve, shifting from sympathetic to parasympathetic dominance.

#### Speech Protocol (Motivational Audio)
- **FACT**: Self-talk interventions are effective for emotional regulation. Key strategies: cognitive reappraisal, compassionate self-talk, and "psychological sigh" technique. (therapeuticcounseling.org)
- **FACT**: Humming breath (vocal vagal stimulation) uses vibration to activate vagus nerve. (therapeuticcounseling.org)
- **FACT**: Maxim already uses "observer protocol" language ("Ah, Subject 13 is experiencing catastrophic rejection sensitivity right now") — this can be strengthened into recorded self-narrations. (memory)
- **INFERENCE**: Recorded motivational speeches in Maxim's own voice would leverage his intellectualization style while creating physiological regulation.

#### Human Bridge (Therapy/Mentor Connection)
- **FACT**: DBT-A (Dialectical Behavior Therapy for Adolescents) is the leading evidence-based framework for adolescent emotion regulation. (impact-psych.com)
- **FACT**: Therapeutic mentoring with a "cool" adult mentor who shares vulnerabilities and models healthy coping is highly effective. (Saltzberg, 2024 dissertation)
- **FACT**: Mentoring shows positive impacts: supportive non-familial adult relationship, scholastic efficacy, reduced misconduct, reduced truancy. (Wheeler, Keller & DuBois, 2010)
- **FACT**: Emotion regulation interventions appear most effective for adolescents who need extra support (depression, anxiety, residential treatment). (UCLA Center for Developing Adolescent)
- **FACT**: Collaborative treatment planning involving the adolescent creates investment and improves outcomes. (Safer Society Foundation)
- **UNKNOWN**: Whether Maxim has access to a therapist or could access one. Not mentioned in memory.
