## What-if checks

**What if Maxim rejects breathing as "just deep breathing — I don't need an app for that"?**
→ Reframe as "nervous system debug protocol." Show heart rate data before/after. Maxim responds to measurable proof.

**What if smartwatch haptics are insufficient?**
→ Use phone visual pacer (color pulse, animation) or simple timer. Still viable.

**What if this works and completely reframes the problem?**
→ Best case: Maxim internalizes physiological regulation so deeply that all other sub-components become easier. Emotional stability is the foundation.

**What if one permission flips it?**
→ If smartwatch access is denied, phone timer works. If phone is unavailable, verbal counting works. Breathing is the most resilient sub-component.

**What if stronger preparation makes it viable?**
→ Already highly viable. Preparation: test smartwatch haptics, measure baseline heart rate, create one 3-minute breathing timer.

**What if this route is bad as a final route but excellent as an information-gathering opener?**
→ It is both. The information (Maxim's response to physiological regulation, heart rate baseline, habit formation speed) is essential for all other sub-components.

## Confidence
**High (80%)** — Strongest evidence base, fastest measurable results, and highest obsolescence potential. Main risk is Maxim's intellectualization resistance, which is addressable via data framing.

## Why kill is justified
1. **Lower psychological fit than Music Ladder:** Maxim's sensual overexcitability makes music a stronger channel than breathing. Music engages his aesthetic and emotional intensity; breathing is utilitarian.
2. **Boredom risk:** Breathing is boring compared to music. Maxim's motivation is unstable — he needs engagement, not just utility.
3. **Can be embedded:** The core function (paced breathing) can be embedded within Music Ladder (breathing during music transitions) or as a standalone micro-intervention triggered by smartwatch. It does not need a dedicated app route.
4. **Music Ladder covers the same ground:** Music listening already produces physiological regulation (cortisol reduction, heart rate change). Adding breathing as a separate route creates overlap without additive value.
5. **Resource allocation:** With 30-60 min daily budget for Project Arete, adding a breathing app alongside music, speech, and human bridge is too much. Music Ladder is the better single investment.

**Note:** Breathing techniques should be embedded as a feature within Music Ladder (e.g., "breathe with the music" during transitions) rather than a standalone route. This preserves the evidence base while avoiding route proliferation.
