## What-if checks

**What if Maxim resists the structured sequence as "too programmed"?**
→ Offer "loose iso-principle": match current mood with first song, then let Maxim choose the next 2-3 songs intuitively. Gradual structure, not rigid rules.

**What if free listening (no sequencing rules) works just as well?**
→ This is the likely outcome for a music-engaged adolescent. The Transition Protocol may be over-engineering. Mood-matched playlists (from Mood-BPM Matrix) with no sequencing rules may be sufficient.

**What if this route is bad as a final route but excellent as an information-gathering opener?**
→ It is primarily an operational protocol. Information value is moderate (reveals transition preferences). The Mood-BPM Matrix has higher information value.

**What if this route works and completely reframes the problem?**
→ Best case: Maxim internalizes the iso-principle so well that he applies it to other domains (e.g., conversation pacing, task sequencing).

## Main unknowns
1. Whether Maxim needs structured sequencing or can self-regulate with mood-matched playlists alone.
2. Whether 15-minute blocks are the right duration for Maxim's attention and schedule.
3. Whether Maxim will follow the 3-phase structure or skip/ignore it.
4. Whether gradual BPM shifts (10-15 BPM per song) are optimal for Maxim's nervous system.

## Confidence
**Moderate (72%)** — Strong evidence base for iso-principle in clinical settings, but uncertain transfer to self-directed adolescent use. Risk of over-engineering a simple intervention.

## Why kill is justified
1. **Over-engineering risk:** The Mood-BPM Matrix + free listening may be sufficient. Adding sequencing rules increases friction without clear additional benefit for a music-engaged adolescent.
2. **Lower necessity:** The Transition Protocol is operational, not foundational. The Music Ladder can function with mood-matched playlists and no sequencing rules.
3. **Intellectualization trap:** Maxim may spend more time optimizing sequences than actually listening, defeating the somatic purpose.
4. **Absorbed by Mood-BPM Matrix:** If the matrix is kept and personalized, Maxim can intuitively build sequences from it without a separate protocol.
5. **Higher friction:** Following a 3-phase sequence requires more cognitive load than free listening, which may reduce adherence.

## Why keep as fallback
If Mood-BPM Matrix alone proves insufficient (Maxim listens to matched music but does not shift mood), the Transition Protocol becomes the necessary next step. It provides the structured guidance for deliberate mood shifting.
