# INTAKE — Iso-Principle Transition Protocol

## Summary
The operational rules for sequencing Music Ladder tracks to shift Maxim from his current emotional state to a target state. Defines the 3-phase structure (match → shift → land), the 15-minute listening block template, 5-10 BPM increment rules per song, target-state selection logic, and emergency bypass rules for crisis states.

## Question this route answers
"What are the exact rules for sequencing tracks to shift from current mood to target mood — including block structure, phase progression, transition increments, and emergency bypass rules?"

Position in family: Depth 5, sub-component 2 of 4 under Music Ladder (route_13f7e0499801). Sibling routes: Mood-BPM Parameter Matrix, Delivery & Feedback Loop, Obsolescence Fade Schedule.

## Assumptions
- The Mood-BPM Parameter Matrix (sibling) exists and provides the BPM ranges for each state.
- A 15-minute listening block is a practical duration for Maxim's schedule (supported by adolescent intervention literature).
- Maxim can identify his current emotional state (or approximate it) within 30 seconds.
- Maxim has a specific "target state" in mind when starting a listening block (e.g., "I am angry and want to be calm").
- The iso-principle (matching current state before shifting) works for Maxim's nervous system — supported by strong evidence base.
- 5-10 BPM increments per song are gradual enough to avoid resistance and large enough to produce noticeable shift within 15 minutes.

## Dependencies and permissions
- Mood-BPM Parameter Matrix (sibling route) must provide valid BPM ranges.
- At least 3-5 songs per mood state in the playlist library to allow for transition sequencing.
- Explicit permission from Maxim: during the protocol, he commits to listening through the entire block (15 min) without skipping songs.
- Acceptance of the "match → shift → land" rationale — Maxim must understand WHY we start with music matching his anger before moving to calm music.
- For emergency bypass: a pre-agreed "safe word" or abort signal if the protocol is not helping.

## What this route enables
- **Reliable mood shifting:** Maxim can go from any source state to any target state within 15 minutes, using a repeatable method.
- **Emotional granularity training:** The act of "I am state X, I want to be state Y" forces Maxim to articulate his emotional experience in structured terms.
- **Emergency de-escalation:** The protocol includes a high-arousal bypass for crisis states where the full iso-principle may not be appropriate.
- **Transferable skill:** Once Maxim internalizes the transition logic, he can apply it without AI prompts — the playlist structure becomes intuitive.
- **Measurable effectiveness:** Each block produces a before/after mood rating, enabling data-driven protocol refinement.

## What could break it
- **Ego resistance to "match" phase:** Maxim may reject listening to angry music when angry, thinking "I don't want to feel MORE angry." Mitigation: explain the iso-principle mechanism clearly — matching is the most evidence-based approach.
- **Song-skipping during block:** If Maxim skips songs mid-block, the shift gradient is broken. Mitigation: short songs (2-3 min) so the shift feels natural, and explicit commitment before starting.
- **Target state confusion:** Maxim may not know what state he wants to be in. Mitigation: offer default targets (e.g., "tired → focused," "angry → calm," "anxious → calm").
- **Time pressure:** 15 minutes may feel too long during high-stress moments. Mitigation: offer a 7-minute express version (1 match + 1 shift + 1 land).
- **Over-use:** Maxim may use the protocol to avoid processing emotions (e.g., always shifting away from sadness instead of sitting with it). Mitigation: include "allowed states to stay in" — sadness is valid, anxiety needs intervention.

## Main unknowns
1. Whether the 15-minute block duration is feasible during Maxim's school day (between classes, transit).
2. Whether the "match" phase feels validating or counterproductive for Maxim's specific psychology.
3. Whether Maxim needs explicit target state recommendations or can choose independently.
4. How many BPM increments are optimal for Maxim's personal physiology (may be 5, 10, or variable).
5. How Maxim reacts to the protocol during genuine high-arousal crisis (can he follow the steps?).

## Evidence that matters most
- **Web research (ISO principle):** The iso-principle is the evidence-based gold standard for mood regulation through music. Match current state → shift gradually → land at target.
- **Web research (transition increments):** Standard increment is 5-10 BPM per song. Studies show this gradual gradient produces measurable mood shifts without resistance.
- **Web research (block duration):** Evidence-based adolescent protocols use 10-20 minute daily sessions. 15 minutes is within this range and fits school-day constraints.
- **Web research (3-phase structure):** Clinical protocols organize playlists into 3 sections: baseline mood songs (slower/minor), transitional songs (incremental BPM shift), target-state songs (intended mood).
- **Memory (Subject 13 psychology):** Maxim's binary thinking (success/failure) means he may judge a protocol as "working" or "broken" immediately. The 3-phase structure provides clear progress markers.
