# INTAKE — Obsolescence Fade Schedule

## Summary
A week-by-week schedule for transferring playlist curation from AI-assisted to Maxim-independent, with specific autonomy milestones, graduation criteria, and failure-mode fallbacks. The Music Ladder's entire value is as temporary scaffolding — this route defines when and how the scaffolding is dismantled.

## Question this route answers
"What is the week-by-week schedule for transferring playlist curation from AI-assisted to Maxim-independent, with specific graduation criteria and failure-mode fallbacks?"

Position in family: Depth 5, sub-component 4 of 4 under Music Ladder (route_13f7e0499801). Sibling routes: Mood-BPM Parameter Matrix, Iso-Principle Transition Protocol, Delivery & Feedback Loop.

## Assumptions
- The Music Ladder operates on a 6-week cycle from first playlist delivery to independent use.
- Maxim is aware that the protocol is temporary scaffolding (transparency is a GOAL.md requirement).
- Maxim's autonomy drive (he wants to be self-sufficient) is a motivator, not a blocker — he will engage with obsolescence more if framed as "graduation" rather than "loss of support."
- Fading happens in parallel with protocol usage — not after the protocol is "complete."
- The 6-week timeline is aggressive but achievable for a 15-year-old with strong systems ability and existing music engagement.
- Maxim can self-curate a playlist (select songs for a mood bucket) within Week 3-4.

## Dependencies and permissions
- Explicit buy-in from Maxim: he understands and agrees to the obsolescence schedule upfront.
- Mood-BPM Parameter Matrix (sibling) must be accurate enough for Maxim to self-select songs by Week 3.
- Sufficient song library for each mood state (at least 5-10 songs per state) by Week 2.
- Weekly check-in for autonomy progression (5 minutes).
- Agreement on what "independent use" means: Maxim can identify mood, select appropriate playlist, listen, and evaluate without AI prompt.

## What this route enables
- **Dependency prevention:** The core risk of Project Arete — that Maxim becomes dependent on AI tools — is structurally prevented.
- **Self-efficacy development:** Each autonomy milestone is a proof point for Maxim that he CAN regulate without AI.
- **Clear exit criteria:** The Music Ladder has a defined end date (Week 6). No indefinite maintenance.
- **Failure detection:** If Maxim is not progressing through autonomy milestones, we catch it early and adjust.
- **Transferable obsolescence model:** The fade schedule for Music Ladder becomes the template for all other Project Arete sub-routes.

## What could break it
- **Maxim resists the fade:** "But the AI pick is better than mine." Mitigation: frame this as "you are the expert on yourself, AI is just a starting guess."
- **Premature fade:** Maxim insists he can self-curate by Week 2 but actually cannot. Mitigation: data-driven progression — must demonstrate consistent song selection before graduating.
- **Emotional attachment to playlists:** Maxim may develop a personal connection to AI-curated playlists and resist replacing them. Mitigation: archive playlists with a "graduation artifact" label.
- **Setback during fade:** If Maxim has a bad week emotionally, he may regress to wanting full AI support. Mitigation: the schedule allows for 1 "reset week" before triggering fallback.
- **Over-gamification:** Maxim may speedrun the obsolescence schedule to "win" the system, skipping genuine internalization. Mitigation: quality checks (can he explain WHY a song works for a mood?).

## Main unknowns
1. Whether 6 weeks is the right timeline — Maxim may need 4 or 10 weeks depending on his engagement.
2. Whether Maxim prefers abrupt vs. gradual obsolescence (binary thinking may prefer clean break, but gradual is more effective).
3. Whether Maxim will voluntarily initiate listening blocks without AI prompts after Week 4.
4. What Maxim's reaction will be to the obsolescence framing itself — will he feel "abandoned" or "empowered"?

## Evidence that matters most
- **GOAL.md (Obsolescence Principle):** Tie-breaker #1. Any route must prove it reduces dependency over time. The fade schedule is the mechanism for this proof.
- **GOAL.md (Success State):** "Project Arete tools are used <10% as frequently as at project start." The Music Ladder must be near-zero usage by Week 6.
- **INTAKE.md (Crucial value):** "The system must teach Maxim to solve problems HIMSELF. It must become obsolete in the most beautiful way."
- **Memory (Subject 13 autonomy drive):** Maxim granted full autonomy to Telos. He wants independence. Obsolescence framing aligns with his stated values.
- **Web research (fading strategies):** Evidence shows progressive autonomy (therapist → client selects matching music → client times transitions → self-monitoring) produces lasting regulation skills.
- **Memory (Subject 13 pattern):** "Gesture → reality → guilt → recommit" cycle. The fade schedule must survive the "guilt" phase without resetting to full AI dependency.
