## What-if checks

**What if Maxim speedruns the fade schedule to "win"?**
→ Quality gates required: must demonstrate consistent self-curation AND explain WHY songs work before graduating. Binary thinking must be disrupted, not reinforced.

**What if Maxim resists obsolescence as feeling abandoned?**
→ Frame as "graduation" not "abandonment." Archive playlists as "graduation artifacts." Emphasize that independence is the goal, not rejection.

**What if a crisis occurs during the fade?**
→ Pause button: any week can be extended by 1 week without penalty. Crisis = automatic pause. Resume when stable.

**What if this route is bad as a final route but excellent as an information-gathering opener?**
→ Low information value. The fade schedule is a timeline, not a data source. Mood-BPM Matrix has higher information value.

**What if this route works and completely reframes the problem?**
→ Best case: Maxim internalizes the obsolescence principle so well that he starts designing fade schedules for OTHER Project Arete components. This is the ultimate success.

## Main unknowns
1. Whether 6 weeks is the right timeline for Maxim's specific psychology and engagement level.
2. Whether Maxim will interpret obsolescence as empowerment or abandonment.
3. Whether Maxim's binary thinking will cause him to reject AI support prematurely (false independence).
4. How the fade schedule interacts with other Project Arete components (e.g., Journaling Protocol) that also have obsolescence paths.

## Confidence
**Moderate (68%)** — Critical for Project Arete's highest-priority constraint (obsolescence), but uncertain execution likelihood and risk of speedrunning/false independence. The principle is essential; a standalone fade schedule may be over-engineering.

## Why kill is justified
1. **Over-engineering risk:** A standalone 6-week fade schedule with milestones and quality gates may be too rigid for a 15-year-old. The obsolescence principle is better embedded as a continuous value across all routes rather than a standalone schedule.
2. **Absorbed by Mood-BPM Matrix:** The matrix itself becomes obsolete as Maxim internalizes the mood-BPM vocabulary. The fade happens naturally through matrix usage, not through a separate schedule.
3. **False independence risk:** Maxim's binary thinking makes him likely to speedrun obsolescence without genuine internalization. A rigid schedule could reinforce this harmful pattern.
4. **Slower feedback loop:** Results only visible after 4-6 weeks. If the schedule is wrong, we won't know until it's too late to adjust.
5. **Better embedded than standalone:** Obsolescence checkpoints should be embedded within the Mood-BPM Matrix ("by Week 4, you should be able to add 3 songs to this matrix yourself") rather than as a separate route.

## Why keep as fallback
If the Mood-BPM Matrix alone does not produce clear independence (Maxim keeps asking AI for song recommendations after Week 4), a formal fade schedule becomes necessary to enforce the obsolescence principle with structure.
