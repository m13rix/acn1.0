# INTAKE — Music Ladder

## Summary
Mood-mapped audio progression protocol using the iso-principle: match current emotional state with music, then gradually shift toward target state. Designed for obsolescence — Maxim curates own playlists by Week 4-6. Leverages sensual overexcitability and existing music engagement.

## Question this route answers
"What is the concrete implementation of the Music Ladder — how do we structure mood-mapped music progressions, deliver them via smartwatch/phone, and make them obsolete as Maxim internalizes self-regulation?"

Position in family: Depth 4, sub-component 1 of 4 under Emotional Regulation Scaffolding. Sibling routes: Speech Protocol, Breathing App, Human Bridge.

## Assumptions
- Maxim listens to music regularly and already uses it for mood management (even if unconsciously).
- Maxim's sensual overexcitability means music will be an effective channel.
- Smartwatch can play music or phone is accessible for playlist listening.
- Maxim will accept initial AI-curated playlists but will resist if they feel generic or "therapeutic."
- Music preference personalization is critical — generic mood playlists will fail.
- The iso-principle works for Maxim's cognitive style: he will appreciate the structured, gradual approach (matching → shifting).
- 15-minute listening blocks fit into Maxim's schedule (during transit, between classes, before bed).
- Maxim can provide feedback on song effectiveness (likert scale) for playlist iteration.

## Dependencies and permissions
- Music platform access (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, or local files).
- Headphones or earbuds for private listening during school day.
- Permission to analyze Maxim's existing playlists and listening history for mood mapping.
- Smartwatch or phone app for playlist delivery (simple playlist link works as MVP).
- Maxim's commitment to try one 15-minute listening block per day for 2 weeks minimum.
- Maxim's explicit understanding that this is scaffolding — he will curate his own playlists by Week 6.

## What this route enables
- **Somatic emotion regulation without verbal processing:** Music bypasses intellectualization and directly modulates nervous system.
- **Gradual skill transfer:** From AI-curated playlist → Maxim-edited playlist → Maxim-curated → independent use → retired.
- **Quick de-escalation:** 15-minute listening block can interrupt amygdala flooding before it reaches crisis threshold.
- **Mood granularity training:** Maxim learns to identify arousal (energy) and valence (positivity) as distinct dimensions.
- **Foundation for speech protocol:** Music calms the nervous system, making Maxim receptive to motivational speech content.
- **Obsolescence proof:** Each playlist is designed to be replaced by Maxim's own curation skill.

## What could break it
- **Ego resistance:** "I don't need a mood playlist like a child in therapy" — Maxim may reject structured music intervention.
- **Genre mismatch:** If recommended music clashes with Maxim's aesthetic preferences, he will disengage immediately.
- **Over-intellectualization of music selection:** Maxim may spend more time optimizing the playlist than actually listening.
- **Social visibility:** Headphones during school may be normal, but visible playlist-following could feel performative.
- **Premature independence:** Maxim may stop using playlists before the regulation skill is internalized.
- **Smartwatch audio limitations:** If watch cannot play music, phone dependency reduces convenience and increases friction.

## Main unknowns
1. Maxim's specific music preferences (genres, artists, songs he associates with different moods).
2. Whether Maxim already has mood-mapped playlists he uses informally.
3. Smartwatch audio playback capabilities (model unknown).
4. Maxim's willingness to provide feedback on song effectiveness.
5. Whether music actually produces measurable emotional shifts (baseline needed).

## Evidence that matters most
- **Memory (sensual overexcitability):** Music is a known strong channel for Maxim. This is leverage.
- **Memory (intellectualization):** Maxim processes emotions as data. The iso-principle's structured, gradual approach matches his cognitive style.
- **Web research (Tuned In program):** 98% attendance rate, significant improvements in emotion awareness (p < 0.01) for at-risk adolescents using music listening protocol.
- **Web research (ISO Principle):** Evidence-based gold standard for mood regulation through music — match current state, shift gradually to target state.
- **Parent route intake (dismantling order):** Emotional regulation scaffolding is first layer removed. Music Ladder is the first sub-component retired.
