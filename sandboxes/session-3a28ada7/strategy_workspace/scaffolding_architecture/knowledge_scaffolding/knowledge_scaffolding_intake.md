# INTAKE — Knowledge Scaffolding

## Summary
Temporary scaffolding for knowledge acquisition and strategy internalization. Provides Socratic questioning, smartwatch teaching nudges, and strategy engine hand-holding. Designed to be the **second layer removed**, after emotional regulation is stable. The core dependency: Maxim currently offloads problem decomposition and strategy design to AI; this scaffolding teaches him to do it independently.

## Question this route answers
"What is the second scaffolding layer — knowledge — and how do we build it so Maxim learns to generate his own strategies and problem decompositions without AI hand-holding?"

Position in family: 3A-ii of 4 (Emotional Regulation, Knowledge, Practice, Existential scaffolding)

## Assumptions
- Maxim will respond to Socratic questioning (he already engages in philosophical self-interrogation).
- Knowledge scaffolding only works if emotional regulation scaffolding is functional — emotions must be stable enough to permit learning.
- Maxim already has strong metacognitive abilities; knowledge scaffolding can leverage these rather than building from scratch.
- The strategy engine itself can serve as a teaching tool (Maxim learns strategy by using the strategy engine on his own problems).
- Smartwatch is adequate for delivering short Socratic prompts and reflection questions.
- Knowledge obsolescence can be measured: Maxim independently decomposes a problem without AI help.

## Dependencies and permissions
- Strategy engine access (already exists) for self-problem-solving practice.
- Smartwatch for daily Socratic prompt delivery.
- Journaling system for recording insights and strategy reflections.
- Permission to track knowledge application rate (% of strategies practiced within 2 weeks — GOAL.md metric).
- Maxim's acknowledgment that Socratic questioning is temporary and will fade as he internalizes the questions.
- Reference materials or summaries distributed via smartwatch for just-in-time strategy recall.

## What this route enables
- **Problem decomposition independence:** Maxim learns to break any personal challenge into variables, constraints, and objectives without AI.
- **First-principles habit:** Maxim learns to ask "what are the axioms here?" before seeking solutions — a transferable meta-skill.
- **Strategy internalization:** The strategy engine moves from external tool to internal cognitive pattern. Maxim runs lightweight strategy cycles in his head.
- **Proof generation:** Each strategy Maxim designs and executes independently is concrete evidence of growth — feeds his proof-driven motivation engine.
- **Bridge to practice scaffolding:** Knowledge without practice is theory; knowledge scaffolding explicitly connects to practice by requiring protocol design.

## What could break it
- **Socratic fatigue:** If questioning feels like homework, Maxim may resist or go through the motions without genuine reflection.
- **Overlap with emotional regulation:** If Maxim is not emotionally stable, knowledge scaffolding becomes intellectualization-as-avoidance (GOAL.md failure state: intellectualization loop).
- **Premature removal:** If knowledge scaffolding is removed before internalization, Maxim may revert to AI for problem decomposition.
- **Strategy obsession:** Maxim may optimize strategy design instead of executing strategies — the "spreadsheet before life" trap.
- **Bottleneck misidentification:** If knowledge gaps are not the real bottleneck, knowledge scaffolding may be useless while practice scaffolding is needed.

## Main unknowns
- Maxim's current level of independent strategy ability (can he already decompose problems without AI?).
- Whether Socratic questioning from an AI feels authentic or manipulative to Maxim (memory: "I don't care about the source, I feel it and it is real" suggests he may accept it if useful).
- Optimal question delivery frequency (daily? per-problem? on-demand?).
- Whether Maxim learns better through text, voice, or interactive conversation.

## Evidence that matters most
- **Memory (strong metacognition):** Maxim already observes his own mental processes with near-clinical detachment. Knowledge scaffolding should tap this, not override it.
- **Memory (meaning-driven learning):** "Subject 13's motivation is heavily tied to meaning, dignity, and self-proof." Knowledge framing must connect to Maxim's personal stakes.
- **Memory (school as battlefield):** School is where Maxim proves he can change. Knowledge scaffolding should explicitly connect strategy learning to school performance as a test case.
- **GOAL.md (Knowledge-Practice Integration metric):** "When Maxim learns a new strategy, he can design and execute a practice protocol to habituate it within 2 weeks, without AI hand-holding."
- **GOAL.md (Obsolescence tie-breaker #1):** Knowledge scaffolding must be designed so usage decreases over time — the questions become Maxim's internal voice.
