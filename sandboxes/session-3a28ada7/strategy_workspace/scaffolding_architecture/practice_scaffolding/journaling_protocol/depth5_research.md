## Web Research Findings
- **FACT:** (Journaling for perfectionism, 2025) Best practice: 5-10 min daily sessions, 5 core prompt types for perfectionism: (1) identify all-or-nothing pattern, (2) challenge rigid logic with evidence, (3) celebrate small wins/mistake-learning, (4) take imperfect action step, (5) self-compassion reframe.
- **FACT:** (Journaling habit research) Consistency > frequency. 5+ entries/week target. Missing days expected, picking up is what matters.
- **FACT:** (MU Extension) Teen journaling benefits: 10-30 min sessions, date every entry, privacy is essential, freewriting and lists and drawings all valid formats.
- **FACT:** (SCS-Youth, Neff et al. 2021) Validated 17-item Self-Compassion Scale for Youth, ages 12-17. Reliability α ≥ 0.82. Six subscales: self-kindness, self-judgment (reverse), common humanity, isolation (reverse), mindfulness, over-identification (reverse).
- **FACT:** (Emotional granularity research) Moving from broad labels ("good", "bad") to precise emotion words ("disappointed", "anxious", "hopeful") improves emotion regulation. Mood tracking with 1-10 scales + emotion words.
- **FACT:** (Prompt fade design) Best practice: start with engaging prompts, schedule gradual reduction ("prompt fade") over weeks, transition from external cues to internal cues. Replace AI prompts with self-generated topics or free-writes.
- **FACT:** (Teen journaling best practices) Let teen choose format (physical vs. digital). Keep journal private. Low-stakes start. "The most useful journal is the one that actually gets opened."
