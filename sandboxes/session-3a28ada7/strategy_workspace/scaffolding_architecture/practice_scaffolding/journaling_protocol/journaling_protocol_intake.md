# INTAKE — Journaling Protocol

## Summary
Structured journaling system with prompts, consistency tracking, and emotional logging. 3-5 min/day protocol leveraging Maxim's existing metacognition. Directly supports GOAL.md journaling consistency metric (>5 days/week). Obsolescence: AI-prompted → self-prompted → independent practice.

## Question this route answers
"What is the concrete implementation of the Journaling Protocol — how do we structure daily journaling, what prompts work for Maxim's psychology, and how do we make the protocol obsolete as Maxim internalizes reflective practice?"

Position in family: Depth 4, sub-component 1 of 3 under Practice Scaffolding. Sibling routes: Visible-Action Planner, Submission Rhythm Tracker.

## Assumptions
- Maxim has strong metacognition (memory-confirmed) — journaling leverages existing strength, not teaches from scratch.
- Maxim will accept a short 3-5 min/day protocol — low enough friction to build consistency.
- Maxim does NOT currently journal consistently (inferred from memory: "no stable default studying routine"). If he does, protocol shifts to quality/emotional depth focus.
- Imperfection is built in — missed days are expected, picking up is what matters.
- Maxim will respond to the "debugging" framing — journaling as system diagnosis, not emotional therapy.
- Physical or digital format works — Maxim's preference TBD but either can be accommodated.
- The journaling serves emotional regulation AND practice tracking — bridges both layers.

## Dependencies and permissions
- Journaling medium (physical notebook, digital document, or strategy workspace note).
- Permission to send 1 daily prompt (smartwatch, phone, or Telegram).
- Permission to track journaling consistency (days/week) — not content, just frequency.
- 3-5 minutes of Maxim's daily Project Arete time budget.
- Maxim's explicit understanding that this is scaffolding — AI prompts will decrease as he internalizes the habit.
- Permission to adjust prompts based on what works (Maxim provides feedback or we observe completion rates).

## What this route enables
- **Journaling consistency metric:** Directly enables GOAL.md primary metric (>5 days/week).
- **Emotional granularity training:** Maxim learns to name and locate emotions (arousal/valence, physical sensation).
- **Action-identity separation:** Journaling creates space between "what I did" and "what it means about me" — key for interrupting the gesture-reality-guilt cycle.
- **Proof through consistency:** Streak evidence feeds Maxim's proof-driven motivation ("I can change, I am changing").
- **Self-prompting transfer:** Over time, Maxim no longer needs AI prompts — the journaling habit runs automatically.
- **Bridge to practice tracking:** Journal entries can include action completions, bridging journaling with the Visible-Action Planner.
- **Foundation for obsolescence:** The protocol is explicitly designed to transition from AI-prompted → self-prompted → independent evening practice.

## What could break it
- **Perfectionism trap:** Maxim may refuse incomplete entries, creating all-or-nothing pattern (GOAL.md failure state: intellectualization loop).
- **Journaling as surveillance:** If Maxim feels the system is monitoring his content (not just consistency), he will disengage.
- **Over-intellectualization:** Maxim may write analysis instead of experience — debugging the journal instead of using it.
- **Friction from format:** If digital journaling requires opening an app and typing, the 30-second startup cost may block consistency.
- **Motivation crash:** If Maxim crashes emotionally, journaling becomes another source of shame ("I can't even journal").
- **Premature independence:** Maxim may stop journaling before the reflective habit is internalized.

## Main unknowns
1. Maxim's current journaling consistency (baseline — 0 or already doing it?).
2. Whether Maxim prefers digital or physical journaling.
3. Maxim's willingness to follow structured prompts vs. free-write.
4. Whether Maxim has tried journaling before and what went wrong.
