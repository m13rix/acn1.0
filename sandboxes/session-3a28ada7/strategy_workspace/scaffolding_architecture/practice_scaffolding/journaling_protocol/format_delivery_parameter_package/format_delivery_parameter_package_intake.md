# INTAKE — Format & Delivery Parameter Package

## Summary
The exact specification of the journaling medium, template structure, delivery channel, scheduling, time limits, and privacy safeguards for the Journaling Protocol. Defines how the journaling session reaches Maxim, how it is structured, and when it happens — minimizing friction and maximizing adherence.

## Question this route answers
"What is the exact format (medium, template, timing, delivery channel, time limit) through which the Journaling Protocol is delivered to Maxim?"

Position in family: Depth 5, parameter package 2 of 4 under Journaling Protocol. Siblings: Prompt Parameter Package, Obsolescence & Transition, Tracking & Metrics.

## Assumptions
- Maxim is highly digital (systems builder, uses Telegram, built a 3D-printer handwriting clone) — digital-first format with physical option as backup is the right default.
- Maxim has a smartwatch all day — can receive notifications/prompts, but may not type on it.
- Telegram is the primary delivery channel (already used by the strategy system) — prompts can be sent as Telegram messages.
- Journaling should happen in the evening (post-school, pre-20:00) to avoid the after-20:00 energy collapse (memory-confirmed).
- Session length: 3-5 minutes minimum, 10 minutes maximum. The parent intake established 3-5 min/day; longer is a bonus, never a requirement.
- Maxim should be able to journal in any format: Telegram message to himself, markdown file, physical notebook, voice note — whatever reduces friction on a given day.
- Privacy is critical (GOAL.md). The system tracks only consistency (did they write?) and optionally aggregated metadata, NEVER raw content unless Maxim chooses to share.

## Dependencies and permissions
- Telegram integration or equivalent for daily prompt delivery.
- A private journaling space (Telegram private chat, local markdown file, or physical notebook that Maxim controls).
- Permission to send exactly 1 prompt message per day at scheduled time.
- Permission to track only: entry timestamp, entry length (chars/words), prompt completion rate. NOT content.
- 3-10 minutes of Maxim's daily evening time.
- Clear agreement that journal content is private: never read by AI or human unless Maxim explicitly shares.

## What this route enables
- **Friction minimization:** Prompts arrive automatically at the right time in the right channel. Maxim does not need to "remember" to journal.
- **Format flexibility:** Maxim can switch between digital and physical based on daily energy. Digital: Telegram message to self. Physical: same prompt, written in notebook.
- **Time-bounded:** 3-10 minute sessions prevent over-intellectualization (GOAL.md failure state).
- **Silence protocol:** On crisis days, the prompt is simply "Skip today. No obligation." — removing journaling shame.
- **Date-stamped consistency tracking:** Every entry is automatically date-stamped, enabling the Tracking & Metrics package.
- **Template evolution:** Format transitions from structured (Week 1-2: template with slots) to semi-structured (Week 3-6: single prompt, free-form) to open (Week 7+: "What's on your mind?"), supporting obsolescence.

## What could break it
- **Channel friction:** If Telegram is not convenient (e.g., phone is in another room), Maxim may not write.
- **Format indecision:** If Maxim keeps switching formats, consistency tracking becomes messy and the habit may not stick.
- **Over-structuring:** A rigid template may feel like school homework, triggering resistance.
- **Under-structuring:** Too little structure ("just write") may not work for a perfectionist who doesn't know where to start.
- **Privacy distrust:** If Maxim suspects the system reads his journal content, he will disengage or write performatively.
- **Timing mismatch:** If the scheduled time is wrong (e.g., right after school when depleted), journaling may feel like a chore.

## Main unknowns
1. Maxim's preferred format — digital (Telegram/markdown) vs. physical notebook?
2. Maxim's optimal journaling time — evening before dinner? Right after school? Before bed?
3. Whether the smartwatch can display a full prompt or only a notification to check Telegram.
4. Maxim's privacy comfort level — does he want the option to share entries, or complete wall?

## Evidence that matters most
- **Memory (after-20:00 performance drop):** "Substantial performance drop after 20:00" — journaling must be scheduled before this window.
- **Memory (3D-printer handwriting clone):** Maxim values both digital AND physical authenticity — format flexibility is important.
- **Memory (no default routine):** "Every session requires fresh emotional activation" — the delivery channel must reduce activation cost to near-zero.
- **Web (teen journaling best practices):** Let teen choose format. Keep private. Anchor to existing routine (habit stacking).
- **Web (prompt fade):** Format should evolve from structured template to open format as internal routine grows.
- **Parent intake (obsolescence):** AI-prompted → self-prompted → independent. Format evolution supports this path.
