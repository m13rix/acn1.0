## Evidence Summary

### FACT: Evening performance collapse
Memory confirms: "After around 20:00 it gets hard to work." This is structural — not just motivation. Practice scaffolding must optimize for early-day action windows.

### FACT: No stable default routine
Memory: "Subject 13 may not have a stable 'default studying routine,' so every session requires fresh emotional activation." This is the core justification for ALL practice sub-components.

### FACT: Automation removed friction, not meaning
Memory: "Once the boring parts are automated, the remaining bottleneck becomes participation, oral answers, practice, emotional buy-in, and consistency."

### FACT: Identity-driven avoidance is primary bottleneck (55% confidence)
Memory: School tasks feel emotionally expensive because they are identity threats. Any practice tool must separate "action" from "identity verdict."

### FACT: Maxim has strong metacognition
Memory: "Surprising amount of self-observation." Journaling can leverage this existing strength rather than teaching from scratch.

### FACT: Journaling consistency is a GOAL.md primary metric
Target: >5 days/week of journaling. Practice scaffolding directly enables this.

### FACT: Summer schedule provides runway
School ended May 25. Free time available for establishing practice rhythms before school resumes.

### FACT: Maxim sustains effort for intrinsically meaningful work
Memory: "Value-driven activation — Subject 13 can sustain effort for self-chosen, intrinsically meaningful work." Practice tools must be framed as self-chosen, not externally imposed.

### EVIDENCE: Journaling protocols for adolescents (Web)
- 3-5 min/day minimum, habit-stacked onto existing routine
- Embrace imperfection — missing days is okay, picking up again is what matters
- Structure: identify patterns → challenge all-or-nothing thinking → learn from failure → build self-acceptance → plan small next step
- Positive self-talk journaling (PSTJ) shows significant improvement in adolescent psychological well-being (PMC study, 2025)
- Prompts for teens: body-based emotion checking (where do I feel this?), time-binding (when did I last feel this?), action-planning (what can I do today?)

### EVIDENCE: Implementation intentions and if-then planning (Web)
- Gollwitzer (2008): specifying when/where/how dramatically increases initiation rates
- Also holds for ADHD brains (Gavrilow & Gollwitzer, 2008)
- Tasks planned with if-then are perceived as subjectively easier
- Core to the Visible-Action Planner design

### EVIDENCE: Submission-receipt rule (Web)
- Define "done" as Work → Submit → Screenshot — all three or not complete
- Addresses the hidden failure point: completing work but not submitting it
- Provides dopamine hit and concrete proof of completion
- Directly applicable to Submission Rhythm Tracker

### EVIDENCE: Executive function skills are trainable (Web)
- CHADD, Child Mind Institute, Baker Center: routines, checklists, task breakdown, visual timers are front-line evidence-based interventions
- Organizational Skills Training (OST) improved organizational skills, inattention, and academic performance in children with ADHD
- 5Cs: self-Control, Compassion, Collaboration, Consistency, Celebration

### EVIDENCE: Habit stacking and scheduling (Web)
- Gretchen Rubin: "If it's on the calendar, it happens." Scheduling locks in behavior.
- James Clear: habit stacking — new habit after existing one reduces decision fatigue.
- Morning routine optimization: consistent wake time, light movement, top-3 goals review.

### INFERENCE: Journaling Protocol has strongest evidence alignment
- Direct GOAL.md metric target (>5 days/week)
- Leverages Maxim's existing metacognition
- Best obsolescence path: AI-prompted → self-prompted → independent practice
- Lowest friction: 3-5 min/day is minimal time investment

### INFERENCE: Visible-Action Planner addresses initiation bottleneck but higher friction
- Maxim may resist "another planner system" (executive function expertise: "resistance to planners is common")
- Frame as "strategy engine in micro" — daily prioritization using evidence-based if-then planning
- Key design: separate action from identity verdict

### INFERENCE: Submission Rhythm Tracker is highest short-term value, lowest obsolescence
- Directly addresses school problem (missed submissions → lower grades)
- But school is finite — after OGEs, this tool has no purpose
- Low obsolescence potential means it must be designed as strictly temporary
