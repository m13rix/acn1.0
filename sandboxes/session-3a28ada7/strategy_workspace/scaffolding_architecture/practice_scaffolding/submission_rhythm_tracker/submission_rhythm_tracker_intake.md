# INTAKE — Submission Rhythm Tracker

## Summary
School task submission management system using the submission-receipt rule (Work → Submit → Screenshot). Directly addresses the missed submissions bottleneck after automation. Strictly temporary design — tied to finite academic timeline (school terms). Bridges practice scaffolding to the real problem: Maxim's school schedule/task completion.

## Question this route answers
"What is the concrete implementation of the Submission Rhythm Tracker — how do we track school task submissions, ensure completed work is actually submitted, and make the tracker obsolete when school submission rhythm is internalized?"

Position in family: Depth 4, sub-component 3 of 3 under Practice Scaffolding. Sibling routes: Journaling Protocol, Visible-Action Planner.

## Assumptions
- The primary school bottleneck after automation is NOT doing the work — it's SUBMITTING the work (hidden failure point).
- Memory-confirmed: "Physics: low marks tied to missed homework." Submission rhythm improvement directly helps.
- Maxim currently uses Autopen + school agent for automated homework — but still misses oral answers, participation, and active practice.
- Teacher impression is a real variable — timely submission improves teacher perception.
- The submission-receipt rule (Work → Submit → Screenshot = Done) creates a concrete end-of-task signal that the ADHD brain needs.
- This tracker is inherently temporary — school is finite (end of term, post-OGE). Its obsolescence is built into its existence.
- Maxim may resist tracking if it feels like "surveillance" — must be framed as "proof of change" (fits proof-driven motivation).

## Dependencies and permissions
- Access to school portals (Canvas, Google Classroom, or equivalent) for submission verification.
- A "proof log" — simple document tracking date, subject, task, and submission confirmation.
- Permission to send 1 daily prompt: "What did you submit today?"
- Permission to celebrate submission streaks (fits Maxim's proof-driven motivation).
- 1-2 minutes/day for logging submissions.
- Maxim's explicit understanding: this is temporary (end of term or when submission rate >90% for 2 consecutive weeks without tracking).
- Connection to Visible-Action Planner: "What is my ONE action?" can always be a submission task.

## What this route enables
- **Submission rate increase:** Directly addresses missed-submission problem (hidden zeroes).
- **Teacher impression improvement:** Consistent submission changes teacher perception (addresses memory: "teachers and visible participation matter").
- **Proof log for motivation:** Concrete evidence of action — "I submitted 12/15 tasks this week" feeds proof-driven motivation.
- **Dopamine from visible completion:** The "receipt" (screenshot) provides immediate reward for completed work.
- **Submission-obedience as identity reframe:** From "submission to the system" to "I choose to submit because it proves I can change."
- **Bridge to OGE readiness:** Submission rhythm for current term translates to exam preparation discipline.

## What could break it
- **Identity resistance:** "Tracking submissions is admitting the system owns me" — Maxim's resentment toward school may block engagement.
- **Surveillance feeling:** If the tracker feels like Big Brother, Maxim will game or abandon it.
- **Tool dependency:** Maxim cannot submit without logging it (dependency trap).
- **Summer irrelevance:** During summer break, the tracker has no purpose — rhythm may break before school resumes.
- **Technical friction:** If school portals are difficult to access or screenshots are cumbersome, the overhead kills adoption.
- **Obsolescence is TOO natural:** School ends → tracker abandoned → but did Maxim internalize the rhythm? Maybe not.

## Main unknowns

2. School portal specifics (what platforms, how accessible).
3. Whether Maxim's "missed submissions" are due to forgetting, technical issues, or identity resistance.
4. Whether school schedule / term dates are still the same (May 25 was the end — need current status).
