## Confidence
**Low (45%)** — Addresses a real problem (missed submissions) but has weak GOAL.md alignment, high resistance risk, and terrible obsolescence potential. The school schedule context is critical and unknown.

## Why kill is justified
1. **Weakest GOAL.md alignment:** Only supports a secondary metric (School Engagement, monthly). No primary metric alignment.
2. **Highest resistance risk:** Maxim's identity-based resentment toward school makes submission tracking feel like "compliance monitoring." Could backfire.
3. **Worst obsolescence:** School is finite. When school ends, the tracker has no purpose — and the rhythm may not have been internalized because no transferable habit was built. Journaling persists after school; submission tracking does not.
4. **Low leverage:** Even if submission tracking works perfectly, it only addresses ONE aspect of the school problem (missed submissions). It does not address identity, participation, or emotional buy-in.
5. **Summer schedule context:** If school has ended (likely, given May 25 date), this route is currently irrelevant.
6. **Embedded in Journaling Protocol:** A "what did I submit today?" entry in the journaling protocol captures the same information without the surveillance framing.

## What would change the kill decision
- If we learn that Maxim's submission rate is below 60% and the primary cause is forgetting (not resistance).
- If Maxim expresses interest in a submission tracker.
- If school is still in session and near-term grades depend on submission improvement.
