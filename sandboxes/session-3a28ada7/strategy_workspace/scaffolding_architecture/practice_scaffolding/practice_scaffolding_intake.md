# INTAKE — Practice Scaffolding

## Summary
Temporary scaffolding for habit formation and consistent execution. Provides journaling prompts, habit trackers, visible-action protocols, and submission rhythm guides. Designed to be the **third layer removed**, after knowledge is internalized. The core dependency: Maxim's primary bottleneck is initiation and consistency, not ability or knowledge — this scaffolding provides structure for starting and sustaining action.

## Question this route answers
"What is the third scaffolding layer — practice — and how do we build it so Maxim develops self-initiation and routine sustainability without external tracking?"

Position in family: 3A-iii of 4 (Emotional Regulation, Knowledge, Practice, Existential scaffolding)

## Assumptions
- Maxim's core bottleneck is indeed initiation and consistency, not knowledge gaps or emotional instability (assuming previous layers are functional).
- Maxim will accept journaling prompts and habit tracking as temporary scaffolding (not permanent surveillance).
- Practice scaffolding can leverage the 30-60 min/day Project Arete time budget without exceeding it.
- The summer schedule (school ended May 25) provides enough free time to establish practice rhythms before school resumes.
- Maxim has enough executive function to engage with practice scaffolding but needs initiation support (not full management).
- Practice obsolescence can be measured: Maxim initiates and sustains a routine for 2 weeks without external prompting.

## Dependencies and permissions
- Journaling system (digital or physical — Maxim's preference TBD).
- Smartwatch for daily action prompts and completion confirmations.
- Simple habit tracker (could be a note in the strategy workspace or a separate app).
- Visible-action planning template (what will I do today, when, for how long).
- Permission to track journaling consistency (days/week) and action completion rate.
- Permission to send "time to start" prompts during Maxim's identified productive windows (morning/early afternoon).
- Connection to Knowledge Scaffolding: practice protocols designed during knowledge sessions should feed into practice tracking.

## What this route enables
- **Self-initiation independence:** Maxim learns to start without external prompting — the gap between "knowing what to do" and "doing it" closes.
- **Rhythm establishment:** Daily/weekly rhythms become automatic, reducing the emotional activation cost of every session.
- **Proof through consistency:** Journaling streaks and completed action logs provide concrete evidence of change — feeds Maxim's proof-driven motivation.
- **Submission rhythm for school:** Practice scaffolding directly addresses the homework/task submission problem (currently the main bottleneck after automation).
- **Obsolescence by internalization:** The habit tracker fades as Maxim internalizes the rhythm; the journal becomes a personal tool rather than a scaffold.
- **Early-day optimization:** Practice scaffolding shifts Maxim's work window to morning/early afternoon (when energy is highest), bypassing the evening depletion trap.

## What could break it
- **Scaffolding creep:** Habit tracking becomes compulsive — Maxim tracks tracking instead of acting (GOAL.md failure state: intellectualization loop).
- **Motivation collapse during scaffold:** If Maxim crashes (emotional or existential), practice scaffolding becomes another source of shame ("I can't even do the practice protocol").
- **Summer schedule shift:** If Maxim gets a summer job or program that disrupts the practice window, the rhythm fails before it's established.
- **Perfectionism trap:** Maxim may refuse to log incomplete days, creating a binary all-or-nothing pattern that undermines consistency.
- **Tool dependency:** Maxim needs the habit tracker to function — dependency instead of independence.
- **Over-scheduling:** Practice scaffolding adds 30 min/day on top of school, potentially exceeding the time budget.

## Main unknowns
- Maxim's current journaling consistency (is he already doing it? At what frequency?).
- Whether Maxim prefers digital journaling (strategy workspace?) or physical writing.
- Maxim's optimal productive window — evidence suggests morning/early afternoon but needs confirmation.
- Whether Maxim has ever maintained a consistent practice routine for >1 week without external support.
- Maxim's actual summer schedule — does he have commitments beyond Project Arete?

This is the core justification for practice scaffolding.
- **Memory (evening depletion):** "After around 20:00 it gets hard to work." Practice scaffolding must optimize for early-day action.
- **Memory (no stable default routine):** "Subject 13 may not have a stable 'default studying routine,' so every session requires fresh emotional activation." Practice scaffolding provides the structure that replaces fresh emotional activation.
- **GOAL.md (Journaling Consistency metric):** >5 days/week of journaling is a primary metric. Practice scaffolding directly enables this.
- **GOAL.md (Sustainability tie-breaker #4):** "The route with lower maintenance burden wins." Practice scaffolding must be lightweight — minimum viable intervention.
- **Parent route intake (30-60 min/day):** Practice scaffolding must fit within this budget.
