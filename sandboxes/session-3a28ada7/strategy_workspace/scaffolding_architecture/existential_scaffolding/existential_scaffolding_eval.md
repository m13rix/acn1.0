## What-if checks

**What if Maxim already has sufficient existential resilience?**
→ Memory suggests he does not: "meaning collapses when instrumental goals fail." The "okay" works during calm states but fails during crisis. However, this may improve as emotional regulation scaffolding succeeds — reducing the need for a standalone existential layer.

**What if existential scaffolding replaces human connection?**
→ This is the GOAL.md failure state: "Project Arete replaces human connection rather than facilitating it." If Maxim uses AI philosophical prompts instead of talking to humans, the scaffolding deepens isolation. This is a high risk given his deep loneliness.

**What if this works and completely reframes the problem?**
→ Best case: Maxim builds failure-resistant intrinsic motivation and deep human connection, making all other scaffolding layers easier to remove. But this is contingent on emotional regulation and practice scaffolding succeeding first.

**What if one permission flips it?**
→ If Maxim has a therapist or mentor, existential scaffolding can be delegated to human professionals while AI handles the other layers. This would flip the route from "AI existential scaffolding" to "AI-facilitated human existential support."

**What if stronger preparation makes it viable?**
→ Preparation needed: identify whether Maxim has any human anchor (therapist, mentor, family member), test whether he accepts philosophical prompts from AI, establish professional escalation pathway before activating.

**What if this route is bad as a final route but excellent as an information-gathering opener?**
→ It IS excellent for information gathering — revealing Maxim's intrinsic motivations and human connection status. But this information can be gathered more safely through the emotional regulation scaffolding layer (e.g., "what matters to you?" as an emotional check-in question).

## Confidence
**Moderate (55%)** — The route addresses a real and high-risk problem (suicidal ideation via meaning collapse). But it is premature: emotional regulation scaffolding may reduce meaning-collapse frequency enough that a standalone existential layer is unnecessary. And the risk of AI-replacing-human-connection is severe.

## Why kill is justified
1. **Premature:** The parent route says existential scaffolding is removed last. But we are at depth 3 — we cannot build the last layer before building the first layers. Emotional regulation and practice scaffolding must be functional first.
2. **High replacement risk:** Maxim's deep loneliness means he may use AI existential scaffolding as a substitute for human connection. GOAL.md tie-breaker #3 says human connection wins over AI interaction. This route risks violating that.
3. **Low obsolescence potential:** The parent route says existential scaffolding "may never fully be removed." This weakens the core obsolescence principle that made Scaffolding Architecture the winning depth-2 route.
4. **Can be embedded in emotional regulation:** Existential check-ins ("what matters today?" "what would be okay even if it failed?") can be part of emotional regulation scaffolding. A standalone existential layer is unnecessary at this stage.
5. **Safety concern:** Without a known therapist/mentor in the loop, AI-provided existential scaffolding for a 15-year-old with recent suicidal ideation is ethically risky. Professional human support should be the primary existential scaffold, not AI.

## What we learn from killing this route
- Maxim's existential needs are real but should be addressed through human connection and emotional regulation, not AI philosophical scaffolding.
- If emotional regulation scaffolding succeeds and Maxim still experiences meaning collapse, we can revisit a standalone existential layer.
- The information needed (intrinsic motivations, human connection status) can be gathered within the emotional regulation scaffolding layer.
