## HIGH-UPSIDE CANDIDATE SEEDS

Beyond the five planned families, consider:

1. **The Integration Model (Hybrid):** Scaffolding as meta-layer, Teacher as content layer, Crisis as safety layer. Highest upsides: addresses all three pillars simultaneously, highest obsolescence potential.
2. **The Apprenticeship Model:** Maxim is not a student but an apprentice — AI as master craftsman who shows, then guides, then watches. Leverages Maxim's pride and desire to prove.
3. **The Game Layer:** Wrap every protocol in meaning/game mechanics that appeal to Maxim's pattern-recognition and achievement drive. Risk: gamification dependency.
4. **The Anti-System:** Deliberately non-optimized, messy, human-centered approach. Force Maxim into real-world messiness rather than elegant architecture. High risk, high transformation potential.
5. **The Inception Model:** Use Maxim's own pattern-recognition against him — design interventions that feel like his own ideas. Leverages his autonomy sensitivity.

## EVIDENCE SUMMARY FOR EACH FAMILY

### 2A. Scaffolding Model
**Strengths:** Directly addresses obsolescence principle (GOAL.md tie-breaker #1). Matches Maxim's cycle pattern (scaffolding could break the gesture→reality→guilt→recommit loop). Explicit dismantling schedule prevents dependency trap.
**Weaknesses:** Requires disciplined execution. Maxim may resist removing tools he built.
**Evidence:** F1, F8, F15, I2 strongly support.

### 2B. Teacher Model
**Strengths:** Matches Maxim's intellectual hunger (F7). Socratic method builds independent reasoning. Observer protocol (F14) is a teaching outcome already partially achieved.
**Weaknesses:** Slower than direct intervention. Maxim may resist being "taught" due to autonomy sensitivity.
**Evidence:** F7, F8, F11, I3. Good fit.

### 2C. Mirror Model
**Strengths:** Low burden on Maxim. Surfaces patterns he may miss. Non-directive = no dependency.
**Weaknesses:** Maxim already has exceptional self-awareness (F7, F14). Diminishing returns on more reflection. May fuel intellectualization loop (GOAL.md failure state).
**Evidence:** F7, F14, I4. Weakest standalone option.

### 2D. Ecosystem Model
**Strengths:** Matches Maxim's tool-building nature (F13). No single dependency point. Easy to add/remove tools.
**Weaknesses:** Risk of ecosystem creep without discipline. No unified obsolescence plan. Tools may accumulate.
**Evidence:** F1, F13, I5. Moderate fit.

### 2E. Crisis-Intervention Model
**Strengths:** Addresses highest-risk domain (F4). Emotion First tie-breaker (#2).
**Weaknesses:** Insufficient for growth; Maxim currently stable (F2). Does not address knowledge/practice pillars.
**Evidence:** F2, F4, I6. Necessary safety layer but insufficient as sole architecture.
