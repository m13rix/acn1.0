# INTAKE — Teacher Architecture

## Summary
Build Project Arete as a teacher, not a tool. It explains the "why" behind every recommendation, forces Maxim to generate his own solutions before revealing hints, and uses Socratic questioning. Pedagogical approaches include Socratic method, deliberate practice, constructionist learning, and mentorship simulation. The AI acts as a wise peer, not an authority.

## Question This Route Answers
"What is the highest-level structural approach to building Project Arete?"
Position in family: 2B of 5 (Scaffolding, Teacher, Mirror, Ecosystem, Crisis-Intervention)

## Assumptions
- Maxim will engage with Socratic questioning rather than finding it frustrating.
- Maxim's intellectual hunger (Dabrowski intellectual overexcitability) is strong enough to sustain engagement.
- Maxim can tolerate not getting direct answers when he wants them.
- The 30-60 min/day time budget is sufficient for Socratic exploration.
- Maxim will not perceive the AI as condescending.

## Dependencies and Permissions
- Smartwatch for spaced questioning throughout the day
- Journaling for capturing self-generated insights
- Strategy engine for problem decomposition practice
- Music/speech system for emotional regulation when questioning triggers distress (safety layer)
- Permission to ask Socratic questions during daily check-ins
- Permission to track insight retention over time

## What This Route Enables
- **Independent reasoning:** Maxim learns to generate answers, not just receive them.
- **Observer protocol deepening:** The observer (already developing) is a teaching outcome — learning to witness emotions without becoming them.
- **Philosophical engagement:** Socratic method connects to Maxim's existing interest in Sartre, Heidegger, Nietzsche, Stoicism, Wittgenstein.
- **Autonomy preservation:** Teaching, not commanding, respects Maxim's autonomy sensitivity.
- **Transferable skill:** Once internalized, Socratic self-questioning applies to any future problem without AI involvement.

## What Could Break It
- **Impatience:** Maxim may find Socratic questioning slow when he wants direct answers (especially during low motivation episodes — "this is worthless" phase).
- **Intellectualization trap:** Could become another system to optimize, avoiding real emotional work (GOAL.md failure state #2).
- **Authority resistance:** If Maxim perceives the AI as lecturing rather than dialoguing, he may reject the entire approach.
- **Time burden:** Deep Socratic exploration takes time. 30 minutes may not be enough for thorough exploration.
- **Motivation mismatch:** When Maxim is in low mode, he may not have the cognitive energy for Socratic engagement.

## Main Unknowns
- Maxim's patience for questioning vs. direct answers (needs empirical test)
- Which pedagogical sub-family resonates most (Socratic, deliberate practice, constructionist, mentorship)
- How quickly Maxim internalizes self-questioning (weeks, months?)
- Whether Maxim's current stable window is long enough for initial teaching phase
- Whether transcription system readiness affects teaching quality

## Evidence That Matters Most
- **Memory (Dabrowski overexcitabilities):** Maxim has strong intellectual overexcitability — thrives on challenge and pattern recognition.
- **Memory (TEL-OS as sparring partner):** Maxim already uses AI as philosophical sparring partner. Teacher formalizes existing behavior.
- **Memory (observer protocol):** Observer is a teaching outcome already partially achieved — suggests teaching model works.
- **GOAL.md §5 (Emotion First tie-breaker):** Teacher model's Socratic method indirectly builds emotional regulation by teaching Maxim to examine his own emotions intellectually.
