# FINAL STRATEGY

## Bottom line
For this user, the best strategy is:

**Pre-verified set seed + short calibration tests + fixed village opener + fast, simple Nether routing + hybrid bow-then-bed End finish, with a bow-and-melee fallback if bed timing is not clean.**

This is the best balance of:
- speed
- stability
- low cognitive load
- low reset risk
- compatibility with a player who has not fully completed the game before

## Why this strategy wins
- **Set seed** removes the biggest early-game time sink: restarting bad worlds.
- **Short tests** matter because the user explicitly asked not to guess their skill.
- **Hybrid End finish** is faster than pure bow-and-melee, but safer than pure bed-only.
- The user’s measured skill is good enough to attempt the fast route, but not so high that we should assume bed-only precision.

## Final route logic
### If tests pass cleanly
Use the main route:
- village opener
- quick portal
- fortress
- blaze rods
- pearl bartering
- stronghold
- End with hybrid bow-then-bed

### If bed timing is shaky
Downgrade the finish method:
- keep bow pressure as the main damage source
- use only a few controlled beds, or none if needed
- accept a small time loss to avoid losing the whole run

### If opener handling is shaky
Spend a little more time on practice first.
Do not launch the real run until the opener feels automatic.

## Overworld opener plan
### Priority order
1. village loot
2. beds
3. food
4. wood
5. iron
6. shield
7. pickaxe
8. bucket
9. portal setup
10. Nether entry

### What to ignore early
- trial chambers
- fancy loot detours
- long mining sessions for perfect gear
- side objectives that do not improve completion speed

### Food priority
Take the easiest immediately available food:
- bread from the village
- cooked meat if it is already free/fast
- quick animal food if it costs almost no time

Do not overbuild the food plan.
The route needs enough food, not perfect food.

## Exact opener mindset
The opener should be extremely simple:
- loot only what matters
- do not full-clear the village
- keep moving
- do not turn the opening into a shopping trip

## Nether plan
### Main objective order
1. reach a safe Nether exit
2. find fortress quickly
3. collect blaze rods
4. barter for pearls
5. leave once targets are secured

### Nether routing rules
- do not wander
- do not fight extra mobs for irrelevant loot
- stabilize a dangerous portal exit before pushing deeper
- take the direct fortress line if one exists

### Practical resource targets
- **Blaze rods:** 6 to 7
- **Ender pearls:** 12 to 15
- **Gold for barter:** enough to make pearls reliable; roughly 16 to 20 ingots is a sensible target if the route allows it

### Bartering rule
Barter enough to be safe, then stop.
Do not overbarter for tiny marginal gains.

**Hybrid Bow-Then-Bed**

- keep a melee fallback ready for low-risk finishing

- faster than pure bow-and-melee
- safer than pure bed-only
- matches the user's current skill profile better than an all-in explosive finish

### End-fight priorities
- do not panic
- do not force bad bed placements
- do not chase the dragon into unsafe angles
- prefer controlled damage windows over risky aggression
