# INTAKE — Random-Seed Scout-Heavy

## Exact route question
Should the strategy rely on random worlds and aggressive scouting/restarts instead of choosing a seed?

## Why this route exists
- It is the canonical seed-agnostic fallback family.
- Some players prefer to keep the run fully random.
- It can be useful if seed choice is disallowed or undesired.

## Assumptions
- The user does not pre-commit to a seed.
- The player is willing to restart and scout repeatedly.
- The route must adapt to whatever terrain and structures appear.

## Dependencies
- Good spawn judgment.
- Fast scouting ability.
- High tolerance for RNG and resets.

## What could break it
- Bad opens force restarts.
- A weak world wastes time even before the run starts properly.
- A death is much more expensive because the route already carries RNG overhead.

## Information still needed
- Whether the user actually wants a random-seed fallback at all.
- Whether seed choice is allowed and preferred enough to make this route unnecessary.
