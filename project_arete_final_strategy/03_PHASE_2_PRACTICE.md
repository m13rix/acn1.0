## 3. THE JOURNALING PROTOCOL (Primary Tool)

### What It Is
A structured 3-5 minute daily journaling system with prompts, consistency tracking, and emotional logging. Designed to leverage Subject 13's existing metacognition. Obsolescence: AI-prompted → self-prompted → independent practice.

### The Evidence
- **Positive Self-Talk Journaling (PSTJ):** Significant improvement in adolescent psychological well-being (PMC, 2025).
- **Bullet Journal method:** "Embrace inconsistency" — missed days are normal; picking up is what matters.
- **Perfectionism journaling:** 45+ research-backed prompts for breaking all-or-nothing thinking.
- **Lally (2009):** Mean 66 days for habit automaticity, range 18-254 days.
- **Singh (2024) meta-analysis:** 2-5 months for most health behaviors.
- **Prompt fading research:** Skills with proper fading persist 89% after 6 months vs. 45% without.
- **SCS-Youth (Neff et al., 2021):** Validated 17-item Self-Compassion Scale for ages 12-17, reliability α ≥ 0.82.

### Why 3-5 Minutes
- Long enough for emotional granularity training
- Short enough to prevent over-intellectualization (GOAL.md failure state)
- Fits within the 30-60 min total Project Arete budget alongside Music Ladder
- Can be habit-stacked onto existing routine

## 5. THE PROMPT SYSTEM

### Prompt Categories (Rotating Schedule)

Based on the evidence, prompts are organized into 5 core categories that target Subject 13's specific psychology:

#### Category A: Emotional Granularity (2x per week)
**Purpose:** Move from broad labels ("bad," "stressed") to precise emotion words.

**Example Prompts:**
- "Rate your arousal (energy) 1-10 and valence (positivity) 1-10. Name the emotion with one exact word. Where do you feel it in your body?"
- "Today's emotion in 3 words: [___, ___, ___]. None of them can be 'good' or 'bad'."
- "If your current feeling were a weather system, what would it be? Hurricane? Drizzle? Fog? Heatwave?"

#### Category B: Perfectionism Interruption (2x per week)
**Purpose:** Directly counter the "do it properly or not at all" pattern.

**Example Prompts:**
- "Write the messiest version possible of what you're thinking. Spelling errors encouraged. Sentence fragments required."
- "What evidence disproves the idea that anything less than perfect is failure? (Hint: look at anything functional in the real world.)"
- "What did you do imperfectly today that still worked?"
- "If a friend had your exact day, what would you tell them about their performance?"

#### Category C: Self-Compassion (1x per week)
**Purpose:** Build self-compassion ratio toward GOAL.md target (>1:2 self-compassionate vs. self-critical).

**Example Prompts:**
- "Subject 13 is experiencing [state] right now. The observer watches without joining. What does the observer notice?"
- "What would you say to a 15-year-old who had your exact day? Write it as a letter."
- "One thing I did today that was hard: ___. One thing I can forgive myself for: ___."
- Adapted from SCS-Youth: "When I fail at something important to me, I try to take a balanced view of the situation."

#### Category D: Action-Practice Bridge (1x per week)
**Purpose:** Connect reflection to concrete action.

**Example Prompts:**
- "What one small imperfect step can you take tomorrow? (Must take <10 minutes. Must be allowed to fail.)"
- "What did you practice today? Not what did you plan — what did you actually do?"
- "If-then plan for tomorrow: When [trigger], I will [specific action] for [duration]."

#### Category E: Low-Energy Fallback (available any day)
**Purpose:** Prevent skipping on depleted days.

**Example Prompts:**
- "One word for today: ___."
- "Skip today. No obligation. (But if you want: one sentence.)"
- "Copy one line from a song you heard today. That's the entry."

### Prompt Rotation Schedule

| Week | Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 1-2 | A | B | A | C | B | D | A |
| 3-4 | A | B | C | A | B | D | Free |
| 5-6 | A | Choice | B | A | Choice | D | Free |
| 7+ | Open | Open | Open | Open | Open | Open | Free |

**"Choice"** = Subject 13 picks from prompt bank.
**"Free"** = No prompt. Write whatever or skip.

### Prompt Evolution (Obsolescence Embedded)

| Phase | Timeline | Prompt Structure | AI Role |
|-------|----------|------------------|---------|
| **Phase 1: Structured** | Weeks 1-2 | Full prompt delivered daily | AI selects and delivers |

## 6. FORMAT & DELIVERY SPECIFICATIONS

### Medium
**Default:** Digital (Telegram message to self, or local markdown file)
**Backup:** Physical notebook
**Rule:** Subject 13 can switch formats any day based on energy. No penalty for switching.

### Timing
- **Scheduled time:** Evening, post-school, pre-20:00
- **Why before 20:00:** Memory confirms "substantial performance drop after 20:00"
- **Habit stack:** Anchor to existing evening routine (e.g., after dinner, before gaming)
- **Duration:** 3-5 minutes minimum, 10 minutes Subject 13um. Hard cap.

### Delivery Channel
- **Primary:** Telegram message with daily prompt
- **Smartwatch:** Notification to check Telegram (if watch cannot display full prompt)
- **Fallback:** Phone alarm + sticky note with prompt

### Privacy Safeguards
- **Content is NEVER read by AI or human unless Subject 13 explicitly shares.**
- **Tracked metadata only:** timestamp, binary completion (Y/N), entry length (chars).
- **Transparency:** Subject 13 sees exactly what is tracked and what is not.
- **Opt-out available:** Subject 13 can disable all tracking except binary completion.

### Template (Phase 1)

```
Date: ___
Prompt: [AI-provided]
---
[Response area — no minimum length, no quality requirement]
---
(Optional) One word for tomorrow: ___
```

## 7. EMBEDDED FUNCTIONS FROM KILLED SUB-COMPONENTS

### Visible-Action Planner (Embedded in Journaling)
Instead of a standalone planner, action planning is embedded in the journaling protocol:
- **Weekly D-prompt:** "What one small imperfect step can you take tomorrow?"
- **If-then format:** "When [time], in [location], I will [specific action] for [duration]."
- **Morning reminder:** Smartwatch displays yesterday's planned action (if set)

### Submission Rhythm Tracker (Embedded in Journaling)
Instead of a standalone tracker:
- **Weekly prompt (optional):** "What did you submit this week? What slipped through?"
- **No separate log:** If Subject 13 wants to track submissions, he does so in his journal
- **Framing:** "Proof log" not "submission tracker" — evidence of change, not compliance monitoring

## 8. OBSOLESCENCE SCHEDULE FOR JOURNALING PROTOCOL

**Based on Lally (2009):** Mean 66 days for automaticity. Range 18-254 days.
**Based on Singh (2024):** 2-5 months for most health behaviors.
**Based on prompt fading research:** 89% persistence at 6 months with proper fading vs. 45% without.

| Phase | Timeline | Trigger | What Changes |
|-------|----------|---------|--------------|
| **Phase 1: AI-Prompted** | Weeks 1-4 | Baseline | Full prompt delivered daily |
| **Phase 2: Self-Prompted** | Weeks 5-8 | 5+ entries/week for 2 consecutive weeks | AI provides category or cue only |
| **Phase 3: Independent** | Weeks 9-12 | 5+ entries/week for 4 consecutive weeks without AI prompts | No AI prompts. Monthly check-in only. |
| **Phase 4: Fully Retired** | Month 4+ | Independent journaling sustained for 8+ weeks | Zero system involvement unless Subject 13 requests |

**Relapse Protocol:**
- If consistency drops below 3 entries/week for 2 consecutive weeks post-obsolescence, system offers optional re-engagement at appropriate phase.
- Re-engagement is Subject 13-initiated, not automatic (to avoid surveillance feel).
- Relapse is data, not failure. It reveals stressors that need attention.

**Graduation Celebration:**
When independence criteria are met, a deliberate "graduation" moment frames this as success. Example: "You no longer need AI prompts to journal. The scaffold is removed because the building stands."

## 10. RISK MITIGATION

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Perfectionism trap ("I missed a day, I failed") | Explicit imperfection framing. "Missing days is expected. Picking up is what matters." |
| Journaling as surveillance | Track metadata only. Never read content without explicit permission. Show Subject 13 exactly what is tracked. |
| Over-intellectualization | Time cap (5 min). Body-sensation prompts. "Messiest version possible" constraints. |
| Format friction | Allow any format any day. No lock-in. |
| Motivation crash → journaling shame | "Skip today" is always an option. Silence protocol on crisis days. |
| Premature independence | Objective triggers required (consistency thresholds), not self-assessment. |
