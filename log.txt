1. **Dependency lock-in** — Subject 13 cannot function effectively without the system after 6 months of use. Frequency of system use increases rather than decreases.
2. **Judgment replacement** — The system makes decisions for Subject 13 rather than teaching him how to decide. Subject 13 reports feeling "like a passenger in his own life."
3. **Emotional bypass** — The system suppresses emotions (e.g., via distraction, motivational hype, or chemical means) rather than teaching processing. Subject 13 reports feeling numb or disconnected.
4. **Motivational instability persists** — No measurable reduction in frequency or severity of euphoria/despair oscillations after 12 weeks of intervention.
5. **System-induced crash** — Withdrawal of the system causes emotional collapse, suicidal ideation, or loss of function. The system created a scaffold that was never faded.
6. **False optimization** — Subject 13 optimizes metrics that don't matter (streaks, hours logged, AI interactions) while core problems remain unaddressed.

## Exclusions (Out of Scope)
1. **Medical diagnosis or treatment** — We are not clinicians. If ADHD, bipolar spectrum, or other neurobiological factors are suspected, referral to a professional is the correct path.
2. **School performance as primary goal** — Grades are instrumental. The system does not optimize for grades; it optimizes for the capabilities that may incidentally improve grades.
3. **Social engineering** — The system does not manipulate other people. Relationship advice is limited to Subject 13's own behavior and boundaries.
4. **General-purpose AI development** — This is a personal architecture, not a product.
5. **Replacing human relationships** — Relatedness must be cultivated in reality. The system may coach social skills but does not simulate relationships.
6. **Sleep/diet/exercise optimization** — These are foundational but generic. The system may flag them as relevant but does not manage them directly unless they are the identified problem.

**Definition:** (Number of system interventions per week) / (Number of autonomous successes per week)
**Target:** Trend toward < 0.25 by month 6

**Target:** Zero system-induced crises. Baseline reduction if applicable.
**Measurement:** Self-report in journaling. System flags keywords.
**Failure signal:** Any crisis triggered by system withdrawal, system failure, or system-induced false hope.

## Tie-Breakers
When two routes are otherwise equal:
1. **Prefer the route that fades faster** — The route that removes scaffolding sooner wins.
2. **Prefer the route that teaches meta-skills** — A route that teaches "how to learn emotional regulation" beats a route that teaches "how to regulate emotion in this specific situation."
3. **Prefer the route that generalizes** — A route that works across multiple domains beats a domain-specific route.
4. **Prefer the route that respects autonomy** — A route that leaves more decisions in Subject 13's hands beats a more prescriptive route.
5. **Prefer the route with lower cognitive load** — Subject 13 is already cognitively overloaded. Simpler execution wins.
